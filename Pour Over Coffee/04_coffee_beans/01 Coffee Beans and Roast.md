# 🫘 Coffee Beans and Roast

No brewing technique can rescue a bad bean, and no bean reveals itself without thoughtful roasting and brewing. This domain follows **the bean's journey** — from a flowering tropical shrub to the fragrant grounds in your [[The Dripper Explained|dripper]] — and explains why every stage shapes what lands in your cup. If [[Brewing Technique]] is how you extract flavor, this is where that flavor comes from in the first place.

## The Bean's Journey 🌱

Coffee is an agricultural product, and like wine it carries the signature of a plant, a place, and a process. The chain runs roughly like this:

1. **The plant** — which species and [[Coffee Varietals|variety]] of *Coffea* was grown. See [[The Coffee Plant — Arabica and Robusta]] and [[Coffee Varietals]].
2. **Terroir** — the origin, altitude, soil, and climate that fed it. See [[Coffee Growing Regions and Terroir]].
3. **Processing** — how the fruit was turned into a dry, storable green seed. See [[Coffee Processing Methods]], [[Washed Process]], [[Natural Process]], and [[Honey and Experimental Processing]].
4. **Roasting** — the heat that develops aroma, acidity, and body from raw green coffee. See [[Coffee Roasting Explained]], [[Roast Levels for Pour Over]], and [[Light Roast and Specialty Coffee]].
5. **Freshness** — how the roasted bean ages, degasses, and is best stored. See [[Coffee Freshness and Degassing]].

## Why It All Matters for Pour Over

Pour over is a transparent brewing method — it hides nothing. A clean [[Washed Process|washed]] Kenyan and a wild [[Natural Process|natural]] Ethiopian will taste like different beverages through the same [[Hario V60]], and a [[Roast Levels for Pour Over|lighter roast]] demands more from your [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]] and [[Water Temperature for Brewing|water]] than a dark one. Understanding the bean lets you read a cup backward to its source and forward to a better [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes|recipe]].

> If you only read two notes here, make them [[Roast Levels for Pour Over]] (because roast most directly affects how you brew) and [[Reading a Coffee Bag Label]] (because it teaches you to decode everything else at the point of purchase).
