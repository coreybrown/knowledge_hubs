# 🍓 Natural Process

The **natural** (or **dry**) process is the oldest method of all (coffee was processed this way for centuries before water-washing existed) and it produces the wildest, fruitiest cups in coffee. Instead of stripping the fruit away as in the [[Washed Process|washed process]], the whole cherry is dried intact, so the seed marinates in its own pulp. The result is heavy, sweet, and unmistakably *fruity*, which is why naturals have become specialty pour over's most polarizing crowd-pleasers.

## How It Works

1. **Sorting**: ripe cherries are floated and sorted; underripe or damaged ones are removed (crucial, since defects concentrate in this method).
2. **Drying whole**: cherries are spread on raised beds or patios and dried slowly for **two to four weeks**, raked and turned constantly to prevent mold and uneven drying.
3. **Fermentation in the fruit**: as the cherry dries, sugars in the pulp ferment and migrate into the seed, building intense fruit and winey notes.
4. **Hulling**: once dry, the brittle husk is removed in one step to reveal the green bean.

> [!note] Where naturals shine
> [[Coffee Growing Regions and Terroir\|Ethiopia]] (especially Yirgacheffe and Sidamo) and [[Coffee Growing Regions and Terroir\|Brazil]] are the classic homes of natural processing: Ethiopia for sunny, dry harvest weather and a tradition of bold fruit, Brazil because its lower-acid coffees gain sweetness and body from the method.

## Flavor Signature 🫐

| Attribute | Natural tendency |
|---|---|
| Acidity | Lower, rounder |
| Body | Heavy, syrupy |
| Sweetness | Big, jammy, fruit-forward |
| Clarity | Lower than washed |
| Typical notes | Blueberry, strawberry, wine, rum, tropical fruit |

A great natural Ethiopian can taste like blueberry jam; a Brazilian natural leans toward chocolate, nuts, and ripe red fruit. These coffees feel weightier on the palate (see [[Sweetness and Body]]).

## The Risks ⚠️

> [!warning] Higher reward, higher risk
> Because the fruit ferments directly against the seed for weeks, naturals walk a knife-edge. Poorly managed drying produces **ferment-y, boozy, vinegary, or "rotten fruit" off-flavors** and inconsistency from bean to bean. What one drinker calls "vibrant and fruity" another calls "fermented and unclean." Trust your own palate. See [[Identifying Defects and Off-Flavors]].

## Brewing Natural Coffees

Naturals are intense, so the goal is to keep that intensity from turning muddy. Use **gentle [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]]** and a slightly **coarser [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]]** to avoid over-extracting their already-heavy compounds, and consider a marginally lower [[Water Temperature for Brewing|temperature]]. A clean [[Coffee Filters|paper filter]] helps preserve clarity. Because their fruit-forward sweetness is the whole point, dial your [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes|recipe]] toward highlighting it rather than chasing acidity.
