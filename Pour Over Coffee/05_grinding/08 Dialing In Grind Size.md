# 🎚️ Dialing In Grind Size

Every coffee has a grind setting that makes it sing — and **dialing in** is the simple, disciplined loop you use to find it: brew, taste, adjust grind, re-brew. Because grind is the [[Why Grind Size Matters|master lever]] of [[The Science of Extraction|extraction]], it is also your primary troubleshooting tool. Master this loop and most "bad cups" become solvable in two or three iterations.

## The One-Variable Rule 🔒

Dialing in only works if you change **one thing at a time**. Lock everything else — [[The Brew Ratio|ratio]], [[Water Temperature for Brewing|water temperature]], dose, and [[Pouring Technique|pour pattern]] — and move grind alone. If you change grind and temperature together, you learn nothing about either. Keep a consistent [[The Anatomy of a Pour Over|method]] so the only variable in play is the one you are studying.

## Read the Cup, Then Adjust

Taste tells you which way to turn the dial. The two signposts are **sourness** and **bitterness**, the tells of [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction|under- and over-extraction]].

| Cup tastes... | Likely cause | Move |
|---|---|---|
| Sour, thin, salty, weak | Under-extracted | Grind **finer** |
| Bitter, dry, harsh, hollow | Over-extracted | Grind **coarser** |
| Sweet, balanced, clear | Dialed in 🎯 | Stop — record it |

> [!example] The loop in practice
> 1. Brew a reference cup with fixed [[The Brew Ratio|ratio]] and water.
> 2. Note the [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time|total brew time]] and taste.
> 3. Sour? Grind finer (also lengthens brew). Bitter? Grind coarser (also shortens brew).
> 4. Change grind by a **small** step, re-brew, taste again.
> 5. Repeat until the cup is sweet and balanced — then **write down the setting**.

## Grind as the Primary Lever 🛠️

When the [[Pour Over Troubleshooting Guide|troubleshooting guide]] asks "what went wrong?", grind is almost always the first answer because it moves extraction more cleanly than anything else. Brew **stalling** or going long? Likely too fine, or too many [[Fines and Boulders|fines]] — go coarser. Brew **gushing** through in under two minutes and tasting weak? Too coarse — go finer. Only once grind is settled should you reach for temperature, ratio, or [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]] as fine-tuning.

> [!tip] Move in small steps and keep notes
> Big grind jumps overshoot the sweet spot. Adjust a click or two at a time, and log the grinder setting, ratio, and time for each coffee. A simple brew journal turns guesswork into a repeatable recipe.

> [!warning] When grind isn't the culprit
> If finer makes the cup *more* sour rather than less, suspect a stall or [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|channeling]] — the bed is mis-extracting, not under-extracting. Fix the pour or [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity|distribution]] before chasing grind further.
