# 🪨 Fines and Boulders

At the two ends of every grind's [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity|particle distribution]] sit the troublemakers: **fines** and **boulders**. Fines are the smallest particles, near-dust; boulders are the largest, oversized chunks. A little of each is inevitable, but too many of either pull the cup apart, because each extracts at a completely different pace from the particles in the middle.

## What They Are

**Fines** are particles far smaller than your target: fragments and powder produced as burrs fracture beans, especially brittle [[Roast Levels for Pour Over|dark-roasted]] ones. **Boulders** are particles far larger than target, often beans that slipped through a [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders|grinder]] only partly crushed, or the inevitable output of an inconsistent burr or a [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders|blade grinder]].

| | Fines | Boulders |
|---|---|---|
| Size | Much smaller than target | Much larger than target |
| Extraction | Too fast → over-extracted, bitter | Too slow → under-extracted, sour |
| Main problem | Clog the bed, slow drawdown | Waste flavor, leave it locked in |
| Worsened by | Dark roasts, dull burrs | Inconsistent / blade grinders |

## Two Different Failures ⚠️

The trouble is that fines and boulders fail in **opposite** directions at the **same** time. Fines, with their huge surface area, dump flavor almost instantly and tip into [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction|over-extraction]]: bitter, drying, astringent. Boulders barely give anything up in a normal brew and stay [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction|under-extracted]], so they taste sour, weak, hollow. Because a single brew uses one contact time, you cannot satisfy both. The cup becomes a confused blend of bitter and sour rather than a clean, sweet whole.

## Fines and Clogging 🚱

Fines do extra damage by **migrating** downward during the brew and packing into the base of the coffee bed and the pores of the [[Coffee Filters|filter]]. This restricts flow, slows [[The Drawdown|drawdown]], and stalls the brew, which then lengthens contact time and *deepens* over-extraction. Stalls, long brew times, and a soupy bed are classic fines symptoms, especially in a fast brewer like the [[Hario V60]]. This dynamic also feeds [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|channeling]] as water seeks easier paths around the clog.

> [!tip] Reducing the damage
> - Use a quality [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders\|burr grinder]] for a tighter [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity\|distribution]].
> - Grind a touch **coarser** if brews are stalling.
> - Some brewers **sift** out fines, or gently pour off the dust, for extra clarity (useful but fussy).
> - Match grind to [[Roast Levels for Pour Over\|roast]]: dark roasts make more fines, so grind them coarser.

> [!warning] Don't aim for zero fines
> No grinder eliminates fines, and a small amount adds welcome body and helps regulate flow. The goal is **controlling** the extremes, not erasing them.
