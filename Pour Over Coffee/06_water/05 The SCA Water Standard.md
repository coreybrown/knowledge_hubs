# 📏 The SCA Water Standard

The **Specialty Coffee Association (SCA)**, and before it the SCAA, publishes a recommended water profile for brewing, the closest thing the industry has to an agreed definition of "good water." It exists because water is the great hidden variable (see [[Why Water Matters]]), and a shared target lets roasters, cafés, and competitors compare results on common ground. The standard is best understood as a **target box** with an acceptable range and an "ideal" centre point, not a single magic recipe.

## The Numbers 🎯

> [!example] SCA recommended brewing-water profile
> | Attribute | Target / ideal | Acceptable range |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Total Dissolved Solids** | ~150 ppm | 75–250 ppm |
> | **Calcium hardness (GH)** | ~68 ppm (as CaCO₃) | 17–85 ppm |
> | **Total alkalinity (KH)** | ~40 ppm (as CaCO₃) | at/near 40 ppm |
> | **pH** | 7.0 (neutral) | 6.5–7.5 |
> | **Sodium (Na⁺)** | ~10 mg/L | low |
> | **Odor** | clean / odor-free | · |
> | **Chlorine** | 0 | 0 |

Those map directly onto the [[TDS and Mineral Content|TDS]] and [[Water Hardness and Alkalinity|GH/KH]] ideas: moderate total minerals, hardness comfortably above alkalinity, neutral pH, and **no chlorine** (which causes off-flavors and is the first thing a [[Filtered, Bottled, and Tap Water|filter]] should remove).

## How to Read It

The famous "**68/40**" pairing (~68 ppm calcium hardness and ~40 ppm alkalinity) is the *ideal* centre, while the wider TDS band of 75–250 ppm marks where water is still considered acceptable. The intent is a water that **extracts efficiently (enough GH)** without **over-buffering acidity (modest KH)**, stays kind to equipment, and adds no flavors of its own. Many [[Building Brew Water|build-your-own-water recipes]] are explicitly engineered to land on or near this profile.

## Limits and Criticisms ⚠️

The standard is a useful launchpad, but it is **not gospel**, and serious brewers treat it with nuance.

> [!warning] Known shortcomings of the SCA profile
> - **The acceptable range is very wide.** A 75 ppm water and a 250 ppm water both "pass" yet brew dramatically different cups, so passing the standard does not guarantee a great result.
> - **It specifies total hardness, not the magnesium-to-calcium ratio**, yet that ratio strongly shapes flavor (magnesium for brightness, calcium for body). Two compliant waters can taste very different.
> - **It targets a general optimum, not your coffee.** A juicy washed Ethiopian and a dark espresso blend do not want identical water; the ideal shifts with roast and bean.
> - **Measurement is fuzzy.** Cheap [[TDS and Mineral Content\|TDS pens]] read conductivity, not composition, so "150 ppm" on a pen tells you little about GH/KH balance.

The honest takeaway: hit the SCA box to rule water *out* as a problem, then use taste (your developing [[Building Your Palate|palate]]) to fine-tune within it. When dialing a new coffee, the standard tells you your water is *sane*; [[Dialing In Grind Size|grind]] and [[Brewing Technique|technique]] do the rest.
