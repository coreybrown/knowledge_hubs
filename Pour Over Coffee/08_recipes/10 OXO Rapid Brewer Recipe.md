# 🟢 OXO Rapid Brewer Recipe

The [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] is OXO's take on the [[The Clever Dripper|Clever]] idea: a flat-bottom dripper with a **shut-off valve in the base** so coffee steeps in full immersion, then drains through a flat paper filter when you press a lever or set it on the carafe. The flat bed and immersion steep make it **very repeatable** and friendly to anyone who finds free-pour intimidating. ⏱️

## Pour Schedule

1. **Setup** — Seat and rinse a flat paper filter with the valve **closed**; add 22 g coffee, level the bed.
2. **0:00** — Pour **45 g** (cumulative 45 g) and stir to wet all grounds; [[The Bloom|bloom]] 30 s.
3. **0:30** — Pour to **350 g** (cumulative 350 g); one gentle stir.
4. **0:30-3:30** — **Steep** undisturbed.
5. **~3:30** — **Open the valve** / set on the carafe to drain (~45-60 s).

## Why It Works

Like the Clever, the OXO is an **immersion brewer that finishes through paper**, so it combines a full, sweet, even extraction with a clean cup and almost no technique requirement. The **flat bottom** keeps the bed level as it drains, which helps even [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]] versus a steep cone. Your two main levers are **steep time** (longer for body and sweetness) and **grind** (finer for more extraction); the pour itself barely matters, which is the point.

OXO also sells a fully automatic pour-over machine — this note covers the **manual immersion dripper**. The bloom step is optional and the steep time is approximate; many users simply add all the water, steep four minutes, and drain. Compare with the [[Clever Dripper Official Recipe]] to see how close the two designs are.
