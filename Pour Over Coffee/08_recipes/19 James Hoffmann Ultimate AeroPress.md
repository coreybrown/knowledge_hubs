# 🚀 James Hoffmann Ultimate AeroPress

After testing the AeroPress's many myths on camera, James Hoffmann landed on a recipe that's almost provocatively simple — and it works. It throws out the inverted gymnastics and the cool-water dogma in favor of an **upright brew, a long gentle steep, boiling water, and a slow press**, producing a clean, sweet cup with none of the bitterness people fear from the device. 💧

## Pour Schedule

1. **Setup** — Rinse a paper filter in the cap, screw on, set the AeroPress **upright** on a sturdy mug; add 11 g coffee.
2. **0:00** — Pour to **200 g** of **boiling** water; insert the plunger ~1 cm to create a seal (prevents dripping). Do not stir yet.
3. **0:00-2:00** — **Steep** undisturbed.
4. **2:00** — **Swirl** the whole brewer (don't stir) to settle the grounds, ~30 s.
5. **~2:30** — **Press** gently and slowly; **stop before the hiss**.

## Why It Works

Hoffmann's testing found that two beloved variables — **agitation and water temperature** — mattered far less than people assume, so he simplified ruthlessly. The **long, calm steep** does the extraction with minimal fuss; the **swirl instead of stir** settles the bed without over-agitating; and **stopping before the hiss** avoids pushing air through a tightening puck, the moment that drips bitter, [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction|over-extracted]] liquid into the cup. Boiling water is fine here precisely because the contact time and immersion are so gentle.

It's an unfussy daily recipe that flatters [[Light Roast and Specialty Coffee|lighter roasts]] and travels well — no kettle finesse, no inversion flip, no pour pattern.
