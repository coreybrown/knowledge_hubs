# 🥣 OXO Rapid Brewer Soup Recipe

The [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] is an immersion vessel with a shut-off valve and a flat paper filter — a "steep, then drain clean" machine. Off-label, that makes it an excellent **broth steeper**: load aromatics, steep with the valve closed, then open it and a clear, sediment-free broth drains into your bowl, like an oversized tea bag. It shines for dashi, dried-mushroom broth, herbal or vegetable broth, and clarified instant miso — the steep-and-drain half of the [[Brewing Soup with Coffee Gear]] brew type. ⏱️

## Pour Schedule

1. **Setup** — Seat and rinse a flat paper filter with the valve **closed**; add ~40 g aromatics and level the bed.
2. **0:00** — Pour 90-96°C water to **~400 g**; stir once to wet everything.
3. **0:00-6:00** — **Steep** undisturbed 5-8 minutes (longer for kombu and dried mushroom depth).
4. **~6:00** — **Open the valve** / set on your bowl to drain a clear broth (~60 s).
5. **Finish** — Season the *drained* broth (soy, salt, a spoon of miso stirred in off-heat) and add cooked noodles or tofu in the bowl.

> [!tip] 🥄 Add miso after, not during
> Stir miso into the drained broth rather than steeping it — boiling dulls its aroma, and its paste would clog the paper. Same goes for any thickener: keep the bed clean, finish in the bowl.

## Why It Works

This is the [[OXO Rapid Brewer Recipe|coffee method]] with savory solids: a full immersion steep for even extraction, then a flat-bed drain through paper for a clean cup. Steep time is the main flavor lever, exactly as in coffee — see [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time]] — and the bed drains evenly thanks to its [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers|flat geometry]]. Because the paper traps fines and sediment, the broth pours clearer than a stovetop simmer, the same clarity that makes the OXO's coffee so clean.

As always with off-label brewing, keep ingredients low-fat so oils don't blind the filter, and rinse the brewer well so your next coffee doesn't taste of mushroom.
