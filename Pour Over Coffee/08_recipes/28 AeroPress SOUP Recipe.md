# 🚀 AeroPress SOUP Recipe

This pulls a **SOUP shot** (a low-pressure, espresso-style concentrate) on a standard [[AeroPress]]. The trick is a **tamped, double-filtered bed** and a deliberately **slow, gentle press** that keeps pressure well under 1 bar, so you extract to espresso strength without the channeling and bitterness real pressure brings. The cup is thick and syrupy with espresso-level body but pour-over clarity. See [[The SOUP Method]] for the full theory. 💨

## Pour Schedule

1. **Setup**: Wet a paper filter in the cap and screw it on; place the AeroPress **upright** on a small, sturdy cup. Add 20 g coffee ground espresso-fine to a touch coarser, level the bed, then lay a **second wet paper filter on top**.
2. **Tamp**: Press the bed flat with a tamper or the back of the plunger; the top filter keeps it even.
3. **0:00**: Pour 82°C water to **80 g**.
4. **0:00-0:15**: Let it **soak under 15 s** (no pressing yet) so the whole puck saturates evenly.
5. **0:15-1:00**: Insert the plunger and **press very slowly and gently for ~45 s**, keeping pressure well under 1 bar; ease off if it firms up.
6. **Finish**: You have a thick, espresso-like shot. Drink it straight, top with milk, or dilute Americano-style.

## Why It Works

Espresso forces water through a fine puck at ~9 bar; SOUP does not. The coarser grind plus a slow, sub-1-bar press lets water flow freely and **evenly**, so you reach espresso strength (~1:4) through high, even [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]] rather than pressure: clarity and sweetness instead of the bitterness and oil that [[Light Roast and Specialty Coffee|light roasts]] often give under real pressure. The two paper filters and the tamp keep the bed flat so water can't [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|channel]]. Tune it like any recipe: a tighter ratio (1:2-1:3) for a denser shot, looser (1:6-1:8) for a longer, tea-like cup. See [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes]].
