# 🍲 Brewing Soup with Coffee Gear

This is the knowledge base's one **off-label brew type**: using a coffee brewer to make *soup*. It sounds like a joke, but it follows directly from what these devices actually are — and the internet is full of people doing it. An [[AeroPress]] is a sealed immersion chamber with a plunger that forces liquid through a filter; an [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] is a flat-bottom immersion vessel with a shut-off valve that drains a clean liquid off a bed of solids. Swap the coffee grounds for savory solids — miso, bouillon, dried mushrooms, kombu, aromatics — and the very same machine that "brews" coffee will brew a clean, single-serve bowl of soup. 🥄

Treat this page like the [[Official Brewer Recipes]] hub: it collects the soup recipes, each written to the same machine-readable [[How to Read a Coffee Recipe|Recipe Card schema]] as every coffee recipe, with `category: soup` in the frontmatter so a future recipe app can filter the novelty out — or in.

## Why a Coffee Brewer Makes Good Soup

Two mechanics carry over from coffee one-to-one. **Immersion steeping** — the move behind the [[The Clever Dripper|Clever]] and the OXO — pulls flavor from solids sitting in hot water, which is exactly how you build a broth from dried mushrooms, kombu dashi, or a bouillon cube. **Filtered separation** — paper, or the AeroPress plunge — then strains the spent solids out, giving a *clean, sediment-free* bowl in a way a pot on the stove never quite does. The AeroPress press goes one step further: forced through a filter, cooked tomatoes or roasted vegetables come out the bottom as a smooth, skin-and-seed-free bisque, the plunger acting like a tiny food mill.

> [!warning] 🧼 Protect your coffee gear first
> Oils and fats are the enemy of paper filters — and of your *next* coffee. They blind the filter bed and leave rancid, savory ghosts behind. Stick to low-fat ingredients (miso, dashi, bouillon, dried veg), rinse everything immediately, and consider keeping a **dedicated set of filters and seals** for soup so your morning [[Hario V60|V60]] never tastes of onion.

## The Soup Recipes

| Recipe | Brewer | Style | "Ratio" | Temp |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [[AeroPress Soup Recipe]] | [[AeroPress]] | Pressed single-serve soup / smooth bisque | ~1:8 base to water | 80-90°C |
| [[OXO Rapid Brewer Soup Recipe]] | [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] | Steeped, filter-clean broth | ~1:10 base to water | 90-96°C |

The AeroPress is the **pressure-and-strain** tool — best when you want to push solids through into a smooth, emulsified bowl. The OXO is the **steep-and-drain** tool — best when you want a crystal-clear broth poured off a bed of aromatics. Both simply repurpose the immersion mechanics mapped in the [[Brew Method Family Tree]].

> [!tip] Read the card the same way
> The fields in these soup cards mean what they always mean — `dose_g` is your dry/solid soup base, `water_g` is the liquid, `brew_time` is the steep — so [[How to Read a Coffee Recipe]] and [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes]] still apply. Want it stronger? Same lever as coffee: more base, longer steep.
