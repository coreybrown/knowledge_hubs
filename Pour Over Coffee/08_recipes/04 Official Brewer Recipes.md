# 🏭 Official Brewer Recipes

**Official recipes** are the methods published by the people who make the brewer: Hario for the V60, Kalita for the Wave, the Chemex Corporation, AeroPress, and so on. They matter because the device was *designed around them*: the geometry of the cone, the number and size of the drainage holes, the ribs, and the filter thickness all assume a particular grind, ratio, and pour. Starting with the maker's recipe means starting where the engineering expects you to be. 🔧

These recipes tend to be deliberately approachable (forgiving ratios, simple pours, round numbers) because the manufacturer wants a first-time buyer to succeed. They are an excellent baseline before you graduate to the more opinionated [[Competition Recipes]] and [[Community Recipes]]. Where a brand publishes several variations over the years, the note here documents a representative current method and flags where specifics are approximate.

Notice how the conical brewers (V60, Origami) lean finer and the flat-bottom brewers (Kalita, OXO) and immersion brewers (Clever) sit a touch coarser. That's geometry driving grind.
