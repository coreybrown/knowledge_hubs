# 🍜 AeroPress Soup Recipe

The [[AeroPress]] is a sealed immersion chamber with a plunger that forces liquid through a thin filter — which makes it, off-label, a superb **single-serve soup press**. Two modes work: a *steeped* soup, where you hydrate miso or bouillon plus dried add-ins and press a clean bowl; and a *smooth bisque*, where you press cooked tomatoes or roasted vegetables through the filter so skins and seeds stay behind. Either way the plunge does the straining a stovetop can't. This is the headline method of the [[Brewing Soup with Coffee Gear]] brew type. 💨

## Pour Schedule

1. **Setup** — Seat a metal disc (or two paper filters) in the cap, screw it on, and place the AeroPress **upright** on a sturdy mug or bowl; add ~30 g soup base and any quick-hydrating dried add-ins (wakame, dried veg).
2. **0:00** — Pour 80-90°C water to **~250 g**; stir 10-15 s to dissolve the base.
3. **0:15-3:00** — **Steep** 2-4 minutes to hydrate the add-ins and build flavor.
4. **~3:00** — Insert the plunger and **press** slowly for 30-45 s; stop at the hiss.
5. **Finish** — Top with fresh garnish (scallion, chili crisp, sesame); taste and loosen with a splash more hot water if needed.

> [!tip] 🍅 Smooth bisque variation
> Load the chamber with ~120 g cooked or canned tomatoes (or roasted vegetables) plus ~120 g hot stock, steep 1-2 minutes, then press. The plunge strains skins and seeds, leaving a silky bisque. Use the metal disc — pulp will blind paper fast.

## Why It Works

Immersion plus pressure does the two soup jobs at once. The hot steep extracts savory flavor by the same physics as a coffee steep — see [[The Science of Extraction]] — and the plunge then strains the solids like a food mill, so you get body without sediment. The cooler water mirrors why the [[AeroPress Official Recipe]] runs cool: it protects miso's delicate aroma, which a rolling boil would flatten. And strength is a dilution game exactly as it is with coffee — press a concentrated base, then loosen to taste, the core move of [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes]].

Keep it low-fat. Oily broths (rich ramen tare, coconut, cream) clog filters and taint the chamber for your next cup, so save those for a pot and reserve the AeroPress for clean, light soups.
