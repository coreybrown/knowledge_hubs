# 🟢 OXO Rapid Brewer SOUP Recipe

The [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] is the **easiest on-ramp to [[The SOUP Method|SOUP]]**: its pump mechanism prevents you from building too much pressure by accident and gives tactile feedback on puck resistance, so the low-pressure press almost runs itself. With a coarser-than-espresso grind and a gentle, sub-1-bar push, it pulls a clear, espresso-strength shot with the vibrant acidity SOUP is loved for. ⏱️

## Pour Schedule

1. **Setup**: Seat a paper filter; add 20-22 g coffee ground between pour over and espresso, and level the bed.
2. **Charge**: Add ~88 g water just off the boil to the reservoir and screw on the pump.
3. **Soak**: Press **slowly, gently, and consistently** until the underside of the screen is **evenly coated** with coffee. That's the soak, so don't force it.
4. **Press**: Once the puck is saturated and the plunger moves without resistance, keep pumping smoothly; pressure should stay under 1 bar throughout.
5. **Finish**: Total time is short, ~20-30 s. You'll have a clear, concentrated shot; drink it straight, with milk, or diluted.

## Why It Works

SOUP reaches espresso-level [[Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)|TDS]] with **coarser grounds and almost no pressure** by trading brute force for an even, unhurried [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]]. The OXO makes that easy: because it physically resists over-pressurizing, you stay under 1 bar without thinking about it, and the coarser bed flows freely so nothing [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|channels]]. The payoff is espresso strength with pour-over clarity, ideal for the dense [[Light Roast and Specialty Coffee|ultra-light roasts]] that resist a 9-bar machine. It's the same idea as the [[AeroPress SOUP Recipe]], with the device doing the pressure-limiting for you. Dial grind for balance (coarser = more clarity, finer = more body) and ratio for strength; see [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes]].
