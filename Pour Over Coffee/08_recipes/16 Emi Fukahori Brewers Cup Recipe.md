# 🇨🇭 Emi Fukahori Brewers Cup Recipe

Emi Fukahori of MAME in Zurich won the 2018 World Brewers Cup, the first woman to do so, with two memorable departures from the crowd. She brewed an experimental **Laurina** (a naturally low-caffeine variety from Daterra in Brazil, processed by semi-carbonic maceration) rather than the near-ubiquitous gesha, and she brewed it on a **GINA**, a valve brewer that toggles between full immersion and drip, while **staging her water temperature** cool, then hot, then cool to shape exactly which flavors each phase pulled. 🌡️

## Pour Schedule

Her documented three-phase routine alternates the GINA valve between **immersion** (closed) and **drip** (open):

1. **0:00**, **Immersion:** valve closed; pour **50 g** at **80°C** (cumulative 50 g); steep ~45 s for sweetness ([[The Bloom|bloom]]).
2. **~0:45**, **Drip:** valve open; pour **100 g** at **95°C** (cumulative 150 g) until ~1:45 to open up complex acidity.
3. **~1:45**, **Immersion:** valve closed; pour **70 g** at **80°C** (cumulative 220 g) for ~45 s to build juicy body.
4. **~2:30**, **Drip:** valve open; final drawdown ~25 s for a clean finish, completing ~2:55.

> [!note] On a single dripper
> Without a GINA you can approximate this with [[The Hario Switch Hybrid Method|the Hario Switch]] (which has the same immersion/drip valve) or a [[Hario V60]] with two kettles, holding the valve or pour to match the 80/95/80 temperature staging. The weights and temperatures above are her published figures.

## Why It Works

Temperature is one of the strongest levers on [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]], and Fukahori used it like a dimmer. The **cool immersion first pour** coaxes out delicate sweetness and fruit without harshness while the grounds are most soluble; the **hot middle drip** drives the bulk of the extraction and develops complex acidity; the **cool final immersion** gives juicy body and rinses through cleanly without dragging out bitterness. Pulling this off requires the GINA's valve plus either two kettles or a precise variable-temperature kettle; see [[The Role of Temperature in Extraction]] and [[Water Temperature for Brewing]].

Her win is also a reminder that **bean choice is a competitive weapon**: a fragile, low-caffeine [[Coffee Varietals|variety]] like Laurina rewards exactly this gentle, staged handling. At home, you can borrow the idea by simply letting your kettle cool a few degrees for the final pour.
