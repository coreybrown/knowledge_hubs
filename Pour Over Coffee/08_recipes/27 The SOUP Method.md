# ☕ The SOUP Method

**SOUP** is a low-pressure, espresso-*style* brewing method: you push water through a coffee puck, but ground **coarser than espresso** so that almost no pressure builds (generally **under 1 bar**) while flow stays high. A gentle soak wets the bed, then a slow, soft press pulls the shot. The result is an **espresso-strength concentrate**, thick and syrupy, at espresso-level [[Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)|TDS]] (~9-11%) and ratios near [[The Brew Ratio|1:4]], yet **clearer and more drinkable** than a 9-bar shot: you get foam, but none of the oily crema. It is especially good at the dense **ultra-light roasts** that choke a real espresso machine. 💥

The name is a playful coinage from the **Espresso Aficionados** ("espressoaf") Discord, popularized by a community member known as *DC* (who notes he didn't invent it) and brought to a wide audience by [[Lance Hedrick Pour Over Technique|Lance Hedrick]]. It's commonly backronymed **"Spro, Only Un-Pressurized."**

## Why It Works

Real espresso forces water through a fine puck at about **nine bars**. That extracts fast but invites [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|channeling]] and [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction|over-extraction]] (harsh, bitter, astringent), particularly with hard, [[Light Roast and Specialty Coffee|light roasts]]. SOUP inverts the trade: a coarser grind opens the bed so water flows freely at **under 1 bar**, the soak saturates every particle evenly so nothing channels, and the slow press maximizes even contact without compacting the puck. You reach espresso-level strength through **high, even [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]]** rather than brute pressure, so the cup lands sweet, clear, and vibrant instead of bitter and oily.

> [!note] ⚙️ Spec at a glance
> | Lever | SOUP setting |
> |---|---|
> | Pressure | under 1 bar (vs ~9 for espresso) |
> | Grind | between [[Grind Size for Pour Over\|pour over]] and espresso |
> | Ratio | 1:2 (dense) → 1:8 (long), **1:4 a great start** |
> | Water | just off the boil |
> | Technique | gentle soak, then a slow, even press |
> | Cup | espresso-strength, clear, foam but no oily crema |

## The Recipes

| Recipe | Brewer | Ratio | Temp |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[AeroPress SOUP Recipe]] | [[AeroPress]] | 1:4 (to taste) | 82°C |
| [[OXO Rapid Brewer SOUP Recipe]] | [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] | 1:4 (1:2-1:8) | 93-96°C |

Both devices suit SOUP because they make the low-pressure press easy to control. The [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] naturally caps the pressure and gives tactile feedback on puck resistance, making it the easiest on-ramp; the [[AeroPress]] gets there with a tamped bed and a deliberately slow, gentle plunge. Either way you can drink the shot straight, cut it with milk, or dilute it Americano-style. Strength is set by [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes|ratio and grind]], just like any recipe.
