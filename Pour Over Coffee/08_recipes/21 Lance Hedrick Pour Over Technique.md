# 🎯 Lance Hedrick Pour Over Technique

Lance Hedrick — barista, former US AeroPress champion, and prolific educator — built a pour-over approach for **accessibility and repeatability on imperfect home gear**. His signature moves are a **coarser grind** (to dodge the clogs and astringency that fines cause on cheap grinders) paired with **aggressive, high-pour turbulence** to make up the lost extraction, plus a habit of **adjusting grind and temperature together by roast**. It's a method that meets ordinary kitchens where they are. 🛠️

## Pour Schedule

1. **0:00** — Add **54 g** (cumulative 54 g, ~3× dose); stir or swirl vigorously to fully wet and agitate; [[The Bloom|bloom]] 45 s.
2. **0:45** — Pour **from height** in the centre to **~170 g** (cumulative ~170 g), creating turbulent droplet impact.
3. **1:30** — Pour again from height to **288 g** (cumulative 288 g).
4. **~1:45** — A gentle swirl to settle and flatten the bed.
5. **~2:30-3:30** — Drawdown completes.

## Why It Works

Hedrick's insight is that **most home grinders make too many fines**, which clog the [[Coffee Filters — Paper, Metal, and Cloth|filter]], stall flow, and add astringency. Going **coarser** sidesteps that, and the lost surface area is recovered by **pouring from height** — water breaking into turbulent droplets at the "stream breakup point" agitates the bed hard, boosting [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]] without a finer grind (see [[Agitation and Turbulence]]). He also adjusts by [[Roast Levels for Pour Over|roast]]: **light roasts finer and hotter** (dense beans need more energy), **dark roasts coarser and cooler** (porous beans extract fast). Above all he preaches that **grinder quality is the highest-leverage upgrade** a home brewer can make.

This is a robust, low-drama daily recipe that forgives modest equipment — ideal if your cups come out muddy or astringent. Pair it with [[Dialing In Grind Size]] to find your grinder's sweet spot.
