# 🔀 The Hario Switch Hybrid Method

The **Hario Switch** is a V60 cone with a little spring-loaded **shut-off valve** in the base. Closed, it steeps coffee in **full immersion** like a [[The Clever Dripper|Clever]]; flicked open, it drains by **percolation** like an ordinary [[Hario V60]]. That one switch lets you blend the two worlds in a single brew — immersion sweetness and evenness, then a percolation rinse for clarity — and Tetsu Kasuya among others has published acclaimed switch recipes. 🎚️

## Pour Schedule

A representative "immersion-then-drain" hybrid (Kasuya-style); valve states matter:

1. **Setup** — Rinse a V60 paper; **close** the valve.
2. **0:00** — Pour **50 g** (cumulative 50 g); swirl; [[The Bloom|bloom]] 45 s with the valve closed.
3. **0:45** — Pour to **300 g** (cumulative 300 g); steep **immersion** with the valve still closed.
4. **~1:45** — **Open the valve** to begin percolation drawdown.
5. **~3:30** — Drains clean; an optional swirl just before opening flattens the bed.

> [!tip] Switch the order for a different cup
> Open early for a more percolation-led, clarity-forward brew; keep it closed longer for more immersion body and sweetness. Some recipes alternate — drain partway, close again, top up — to layer the two styles.

## Why It Works

The valve hands you **time as a direct lever**. While closed, every ground sits in the same water for the same duration — the hallmark even, forgiving extraction of [[French Press|immersion]]. When you open it, the brew finishes as [[The Drawdown|percolation]], the paper filter polishing out fines for a clean finish. You get immersion's roundness *and* paper's clarity, and you can bias toward either by **when you flip the switch**. It's the most flexible single dripper in the book, which is why it became a competition and café favorite.

This sits alongside the [[Clever Dripper Official Recipe]] and [[OXO Rapid Brewer Recipe]] in the immersion family, but with the added percolation phase. Treat the timings as a baseline; the switch invites experimentation.
