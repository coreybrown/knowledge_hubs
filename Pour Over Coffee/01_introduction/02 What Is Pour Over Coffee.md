# ☕ What Is Pour Over Coffee?

**Pour over** is a method of brewing coffee in which hot water is poured, by hand, over a bed of ground coffee held in a filter-lined [[The Dripper Explained|dripper]]. Gravity pulls the water down through the coffee and the [[Coffee Filters — Paper, Metal, and Cloth|filter]], extracting soluble flavor as it goes, and the finished brew drips into a vessel below. It belongs to the broader family of **filter** or **drip** coffee — see the [[Brew Method Family Tree]].

## How It Works

A pour over is a **percolation** brew: fresh water continuously passes through the coffee bed rather than soaking in it. Four things define the result:

1. **The ratio** — how much water per gram of coffee, usually around 15:1 to 17:1. See [[The Brew Ratio]].
2. **The grind** — surface area controls how fast flavor dissolves. See [[Grind Size for Pour Over]].
3. **The water** — its [[Water Temperature for Brewing|temperature]] and [[Water Chemistry — Hardness and Alkalinity|mineral content]].
4. **The pour** — how, when, and where water is added, governing [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]] and even [[Bypass and Channeling|extraction]].

Master those and you control [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]] — the heart of every good cup, explained in [[The Science of Extraction]].

## Pour Over vs. Immersion

Pour over is often contrasted with **immersion** brewing, where coffee steeps fully submerged in water before being separated — as in a [[French Press]] or [[The Clever Dripper|Clever Dripper]]. The [[AeroPress]] and [[OXO Rapid Brewer]] straddle both worlds. Percolation tends to yield a cleaner, more delineated cup; immersion a heavier, more forgiving one. The [[Brew Method Comparison Table]] lays the trade-offs side by side.

## What Pour Over Is *Not*

- Not just "drip coffee from a machine" — the defining feature is **manual control** of the pour, though automatic pour-over brewers exist.
- Not inherently weak — strength is set by the [[The Brew Ratio|ratio]], not the method.
- Not a single recipe — it is a family of techniques across many [[Equipment and Drippers|drippers]], from the [[Hario V60]] to the [[Chemex]].

## Why People Love It ❤️

Pour over rewards attention. With nothing but a dripper, a filter, and a [[Gooseneck Kettles|kettle]], a brewer can coax strikingly different cups from the same coffee by adjusting a single variable. It is cheap to start, [[Pour Over Quick Start Checklist|easy to begin]], and deep enough to sustain a lifetime of refinement — which is why it became the signature ritual of the [[The Specialty Coffee Movement|specialty coffee movement]].
