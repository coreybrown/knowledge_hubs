# 🍶 Hario V60

The **Hario V60** is the most iconic pour over dripper in the world — the brewer that, more than any other, defines the modern image of manual coffee. Launched by Japanese glassware maker Hario in 2004, its name comes from its shape: a **V**-shaped cone at a **60**-degree angle. It is a pure percolation brewer that hands almost all control to the user, rewarding skill with extraordinary clarity. For its history see [[Hario and the V60]]; here we cover the hardware.

## Specs at a Glance

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| Material | Ceramic, plastic, glass, or metal (see [[Dripper Materials and Heat Retention]]) |
| Geometry | 60° cone — [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers\|conical]] |
| Holes | One large central hole |
| Ribs | Tall spiral ribs running up the cone |
| Filter | Cone-shaped paper ([[Coffee Filters — Paper, Metal, and Cloth\|01/02/03 sizes]]) |
| Flow speed | Fast — grind and pour control the rate |
| Cup style | Clean, bright, articulate, high clarity |

## Why the Design Works

Three features make the V60 a control instrument. The **single large hole** means the dripper itself barely restricts flow — the *coffee bed* and *your pour* set the speed, so a finer [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]] or a slower pour directly lengthens contact time. The **spiral ribs** hold the filter off the wall and curve, encouraging air to escape and water to drain freely down the whole cone. The **steep 60° cone** funnels coffee into a deep central column, concentrating extraction at the point where the brewer's [[Pouring Technique|pour]] has the most leverage.

> [!tip] The V60 rewards (and punishes) technique
> Because nothing restricts the flow for you, the V60 magnifies both good and bad pouring. It is the most expressive dripper and the least forgiving — which is exactly why it dominates competition and obsessive home brewing.

## Sizes 01 / 02 / 03

- **01** — single cup (~1–2 cups), small dose.
- **02** — the standard; brews 1–4 cups and the default for most recipes. Buy this one first.
- **03** — large batch, up to ~6 cups; needs a confident, high pour rate.

Filters are size-specific — an 02 filter does not fit an 01 cone — so match your paper to your dripper.
