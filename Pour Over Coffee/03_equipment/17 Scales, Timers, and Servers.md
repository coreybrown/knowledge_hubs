# ⚖️ Scales, Timers, and Servers

The dripper and kettle get the glory, but **scales, timers, and servers** are what turn a lucky cup into a *repeatable* one. They are cheap, unglamorous, and quietly transformative: weighing your dose and water, watching the clock, and catching the brew in the right vessel are the difference between a recipe you can reproduce and one you can only hope for. Together with the dripper, filter, kettle, and grinder, they complete the [[Equipment and Drippers|core kit]].

## Precision Scales

A coffee scale measures **dose and water to 0.1 g**, the resolution needed to hit a [[The Brew Ratio|brew ratio]] precisely. The best ones include a **built-in timer**, so you can track [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time|contact time]] and pour weight on one display.

> [!tip] What to look for in a scale
> - **0.1 g resolution** and a flat, stable platform big enough for your server.
> - A **built-in timer** (ideally auto-start when the first drops land).
> - **Fast response** — cheap scales lag, making controlled pouring guesswork.
> - **Water resistance** — spills are inevitable.

Weighing beats measuring by volume: a "scoop" of [[Coffee Freshness and Degassing|fresh, light-roast]] beans varies wildly in mass, while grams don't lie. This is the foundation of [[Dialing In Grind Size|dialing in]] and following any [[Recipes and Methods|recipe]].

## Timers

Timing governs the [[The Bloom|bloom]], the pour schedule, and the [[The Drawdown|drawdown]] — all of which set [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time|total contact time]]. A built-in scale timer is ideal, but a phone stopwatch works. Many [[Competition Recipes|competition recipes]] like the [[Tetsu Kasuya 4:6 Method]] are written entirely around the clock.

## Servers and Decanters

The **server** (or decanter) catches the brew beneath the dripper. Options range from a simple mug to a graduated glass carafe.

| Server | Best for |
|---|---|
| Mug | Single cup, minimal gear |
| Glass range server | [[Single Cup vs Batch Pour Over\|1–4 cups]], lets you see volume |
| Carafe / decanter | [[Batch Pour Over Recipe\|Batch]] brewing, serving guests |

The [[Chemex]] famously **is** its own server — dripper and decanter in one piece of glass.

> [!note] Small accessories that complete a setup
> A **distribution tool** or [[The Rao Spin|spin]] technique to level the bed, a **filter holder**, and a **drip tray** round out a station. None are essential, but each smooths the ritual.

> [!warning] Don't over-buy
> Gadgets multiply endlessly in coffee. A 0.1 g scale, a phone timer, and any vessel will brew world-class coffee. Buy accessories to fix a *specific* problem, not to chase completeness.
