# ⚡ Flat Bottom Drippers — April and Orea

While the [[Kalita Wave]] defined the classic flat bottom, a new generation of **fast-flowing flat brewers** has emerged from the specialty scene — led by the Danish roaster April and the British-designed Orea. These drippers keep the flat bed's **even, sweet extraction** but pair it with very high flow rates and standard flat-bottom filters, giving the modern competitor the best of both worlds. They represent the contemporary flat scene that has quietly taken over many café bars.

## The Two Flagships

> [!example] April vs Orea
> The **April Brewer** (from April Coffee Roasters in Copenhagen) is a tall, steep-walled flat dripper prized for clarity-with-sweetness. The **Orea Brewer** (V3/V4) uses a near-flat base with a wide-open bottom and minimal restriction for extremely fast, controllable flow.

## Specs at a Glance

| Property | April | Orea |
|---|---|---|
| Geometry | [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers\|Flat]], steep walls | Flat, very open base |
| Holes | Open central drainage | Wide open bottom |
| Ribs | Vertical ribs | Ribbed flow channels |
| Filter | Flat-bottom paper (155/185 wave or generic) | Flat-bottom paper |
| Flow speed | Fast | Very fast |
| Cup style | Sweet, clean, structured | Even, high-clarity, sweet |

## Why Fast Flow Matters

Older flat drippers like the Wave deliberately **restrict** flow with small holes. The modern approach instead removes restriction and lets the **grind and pour** govern speed — the same philosophy as the [[Hario V60]], applied to a flat bed. Faster flow lets brewers use a **finer** [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]] and more [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]] to raise [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction]] without over-steeping, chasing high extraction *and* high clarity at once. This is why these brewers feature in cutting-edge [[Competition Recipes|competition routines]].

> [!tip] These reward a good grinder
> Fast flat brewers expose grind quality. With a uniform grind (see [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity]]) they produce remarkably sweet, layered cups; with a poor grinder they can run thin. Pair them with a capable [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders|burr grinder]].

> [!warning] Hype outpaces evidence
> The flat-fast category is fashionable, and marketing claims of dramatically "higher extraction" should be read with caution. They make excellent coffee — but a well-dialed [[Kalita Wave]] or [[Hario V60]] is not far behind. Buy for the workflow you enjoy.

## Recipes

These flat brewers run flat-bottom recipes; adapt the [[Kalita Wave Official Recipe]] or flat-friendly [[Community Recipes|community recipes]], using a finer grind to suit their faster drainage.
