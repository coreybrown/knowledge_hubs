# 🔁 OXO Rapid Brewer

The **OXO Rapid Brewer** (also called the OXO Brew Rapid Brewer) is a recent **immersion-and-drain hybrid** that aims to make a flat-bottom, paper-clean cup almost foolproof. Like the [[The Clever Dripper|Clever Dripper]], it steeps the coffee bed and then **releases** the brew through a filter, but it pairs that mechanism with a **flat bottom** and a wide, even drain for repeatable, forgiving cups. It sits squarely in the family of brewers that blur the line between pour over and immersion, alongside the Clever and the [[AeroPress]].

## Specs at a Glance

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| Material | Plastic with stainless accents |
| Geometry | [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers\|Flat bottom]] basket |
| Mechanism | **Immersion** (steep) then **release** through a flat filter |
| Filter | Flat-bottom paper (Kalita-wave-style) |
| Flow speed | Steep-controlled, then even drain |
| Cup style | Sweet, even, clean, very repeatable |

## How It Works

> [!example] Steep, then release
> Add grounds and a flat-bottom filter, pour your water, and the bed **steeps** like an immersion brew. When you're ready, a release mechanism opens and the brew drains evenly through the paper into your cup or server.

Because the coffee **submerges** during the steep, the brewer sidesteps the pour-over pitfalls of [[Bypass and Channeling|channeling]] and uneven extraction; your [[Pouring Technique|pour]] barely matters. The **flat bottom** then drains uniformly across the whole bed, reinforcing the even extraction that flats are known for (see [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers]]). The result is a sweet, balanced, highly **repeatable** cup with minimal skill required.

## How It Differs

> [!note] OXO vs pure pour over vs Clever
> **Vs pure pour over** (like the [[Hario V60]]): the OXO *steeps* rather than percolates, so it's far more forgiving: there's no continuous pour to get right. **Vs the [[The Clever Dripper|Clever]]:** both steep-and-release, but the OXO uses a **flat bottom** for even drainage where the Clever uses a **cone**, and the two differ in release ergonomics. The OXO leans toward repeatable batches and hands-off convenience.

> [!tip] Best for repeatability
> If you want the same good cup every morning with little technique, the OXO is purpose-built for it. Dial flavor with **steep time** and [[The Brew Ratio|ratio]] rather than pour skill.
