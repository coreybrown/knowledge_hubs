# ⚖️ The Brew Ratio

The **brew ratio** is the proportion of coffee to water: the single number that most directly sets how **strong** your cup will be. Written as `coffee:water` (e.g. `1:16`), it tells you how many grams of water you pour per gram of coffee. Get this right and everything else ([[Pouring Technique|pour]], [[The Bloom|bloom]], [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]]) fine-tunes around it.

## How to Express It

> [!note] Two ways, one idea
> `1:16` means **16 g of water per 1 g of coffee**. Some people flip it and say "16:1" or even "60 g/L." They all describe the same concentration.

For a single cup: 15 g coffee × 16 = 240 g water. A [[Scales, Timers, and Servers|scale]] makes this trivial and repeatable. Eyeballing scoops is the fastest way to an inconsistent cup.

## The "Golden Ratio" Range

The [[The Specialty Coffee Industry|specialty]] world centers on a band rather than a single magic number:

| Ratio | Roughly | Cup character |
|---|---|---|
| 1:14–1:15 | Strong | Bold, heavy [[Sweetness and Body\|body]], intense |
| 1:16 | Balanced | The common all-rounder default |
| 1:17–1:18 | Light | Delicate, tea-like, more [[Acidity in Coffee\|clarity]] |

> [!warning] "Golden ratio" is a guideline, not a law
> The oft-quoted 1:18 "golden ratio" comes from drip-machine standards. Modern pour-over brewers commonly run stronger (1:15 to 1:16), and there is no single correct value. Pick what tastes good to *you*.

## Ratio Sets Strength, Not Extraction

A crucial distinction: ratio controls **strength** (how concentrated the brew is, its [[Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)|TDS]]), while [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]], [[Water Temperature for Brewing|temperature]], and [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]] control **[[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction yield]]** (how much you pull from the grounds). You can have a strong-but-underextracted cup or a weak-but-overextracted one. The [[The Coffee Brewing Control Chart|brewing control chart]] plots these two axes against each other.

## Adjusting in Practice

- **Too weak / watery?** Use *more coffee* (lower the water number, e.g. 1:16 → 1:15) before you reach for a finer grind.
- **Too intense / muddy?** Add water (1:15 → 1:17) for a lighter, cleaner cup.
- **Changing dose?** Keep the ratio fixed and scale both numbers together so the strength stays put; see [[Recipe Variables and Cup Outcomes]].

Lighter roasts often reward a slightly higher coffee dose; see [[Roast Levels for Pour Over]]. When you scale up to a [[Single Cup vs Batch Pour Over|batch]], the ratio holds even as bed depth and time change.
