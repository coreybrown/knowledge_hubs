# ⏬ The Drawdown

The **drawdown** is the final phase of a brew: after your last pour, the remaining water drains down through the coffee bed and out of the [[The Dripper Explained|dripper]]. It is the most diagnostic moment in pour over — how the bed drains tells you almost everything about whether your grind, pour, and agitation were balanced. Learn to read it and you barely need a recipe.

## What Happens During Drawdown

As water exits, the coffee bed settles and **compacts**. [[Fines and Boulders|Fines]] migrate downward and collect against the [[Coffee Filters — Paper, Metal, and Cloth|filter]], where they can throttle the flow. The slurry level drops, the surface flattens (or craters), and the last water passes through the most extracted grounds. When dripping stops, the brew is finished and your total [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time|contact time]] is set.

## Reading Drawdown Time 🔍

> [!example] Drawdown is a gauge, not a target
> You do not "aim" for a drawdown time directly — it is an *output* that reveals what your inputs did. A surprisingly fast or slow drawdown is a clue, not a verdict.

| Drawdown | Likely cause | Likely cup |
|---|---|---|
| Too fast | [[Grind Size for Pour Over\|Grind]] too coarse, [[Bypass and Channeling\|channeling]] | Weak, sour, [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction\|under-extracted]] |
| On target | Balanced grind & pour | Sweet, balanced |
| Too slow / stalled | Grind too fine, fines clogging, over-agitation | Bitter, [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction\|over-extracted]] muddy |

## The Bed You're Left With

> [!tip] Read the spent grounds
> Lift the dripper and look. A **flat, level bed** means even flow — well done. A deep **crater** or a **"donut"** with raised edges means you poured unevenly. Coffee stranded high on the [[Coffee Filters — Paper, Metal, and Cloth|filter]] walls is a sign of [[Bypass and Channeling|bypass]].

## When Drawdown Stalls

A stalled drawdown — water sitting and refusing to drain — usually means too many [[Fines and Boulders|fines]] have sealed the filter, often from too fine a [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]] or too much [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]]. Fixes: grind coarser, agitate less, or switch to a [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity|more uniform]] grinder. A [[Kalita Wave|Kalita Wave]] or other [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers|flat-bottom]] brewer drains more steadily and is more forgiving of fines than a steep [[Hario V60|V60]] cone.

## Speeding It Up or Slowing It Down

Your grind is the master control on drawdown speed: coarser drains faster, finer slower. Pour style matters too — keeping the bed flooded (continuous) slows things, while [[Pulse vs Continuous Pouring|pulsing]] lets it drain and reset. The [[The Rao Spin|Rao spin]] flattens the bed for a cleaner, more uniform final drain.
