# 🕳️ Bypass and Channeling

Two related faults explain most disappointing pour overs: **bypass**, where water flows around the coffee instead of through it, and **channeling**, where water carves a preferred path through the bed and floods down it. Both mean some water under-extracts while it races past, leaving you a cup that is simultaneously **weak and harsh** — the hallmark of uneven extraction.

## Bypass: Water Skips the Coffee

Bypass happens when water reaches the filter or the dripper wall and drains *without* passing through much coffee. Common culprits:

- Pouring onto the [[Coffee Filters — Paper, Metal, and Cloth|filter]] wall, washing grounds upward and opening a gap.
- Coffee stranded high on the walls during [[The Drawdown|drawdown]], left dry.
- A bed that is too shallow for its volume — see [[Single Cup vs Batch Pour Over]].

Some bypass is designed in: [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers|conical]] drippers like the [[Hario V60|V60]] allow a little wall flow by nature, which is part of their clean character. The goal is to control it, not eliminate it.

## Channeling: A Path of Least Resistance

> [!warning] One channel ruins the whole brew
> A channel is a low-resistance crack in the bed. Once it forms, water rushes through it preferentially, over-extracting that narrow column while the rest of the bed barely brews. The cup tastes thin, sour, *and* bitter at once.

Channeling is the percolation cousin of the same fault in espresso, and the science is laid out in [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction]].

## What Causes Channeling? 🔎

| Cause | Why it channels | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Uneven bed | Water finds the thin spot | Level the bed before brewing |
| Aggressive pour | [[Agitation and Turbulence\|Turbulence]] drills a hole | Pour gentler, lower |
| Dry pockets | Unwet clumps divert flow | Better [[The Bloom\|bloom]], stir gently |
| Too many [[Fines and Boulders\|fines]] | Migrate and crack the bed | [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity\|Uniform grind]] |
| Pouring on the wall | Opens a wall channel | Keep pours inside the bed |

## Prevention 🛡️

> [!tip] An even bed is the whole game
> Start level: dose, tap the dripper, and consider wetting evenly during the [[The Bloom|bloom]]. Pour in calm spirals that stay off the walls. Finish with a gentle swirl — the [[The Rao Spin|Rao spin]] — to settle grounds back into a flat bed for an even drawdown.

A [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity|uniform grind]] from a quality [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders|burr grinder]] is the deepest defense: fewer fines means fewer migrating particles to crack the bed. When a cup is off and you suspect channeling, confirm with the [[Pour Over Troubleshooting Guide]].
