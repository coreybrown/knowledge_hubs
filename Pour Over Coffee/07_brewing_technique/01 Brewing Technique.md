# 🌀 Brewing Technique

**Brewing technique** is everything the brewer actually *does* at the dripper, the controllable levers of a manual brew. If [[Grinding|grind]], [[Water for Coffee|water]], and [[Coffee Beans and Roast|coffee]] are the ingredients, technique is the cooking: how you wet the bed, when and where you pour, how much energy you add, and how you read the brew as it drains. This domain is about the *principles* behind those moves. The specific, timed pour schedules (Hoffmann's V60, Kasuya's [[Tetsu Kasuya 4:6 Method|4:6]], the [[Tetsu Kasuya 4:6 Method|4:6 method]]) live in the [[Recipes and Methods|recipes domain]] and apply these principles to a cup.

## The Levers You Control ⚙️

Every pour over comes down to a handful of variables you can move independently. Master each one and you can steer a cup deliberately rather than by luck.

## Technique in Context

Technique never works alone. The same pour gives a different cup depending on your [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]], your [[Water Temperature for Brewing|water temperature]], and your [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers|dripper geometry]]. When a brew goes wrong, the cause is usually one lever out of balance. Diagnose it with the [[Pour Over Troubleshooting Guide]] and confirm with the [[Under-Extraction and Over-Extraction|extraction]] lens. Technique is where theory meets the cup, so keep [[The Science of Extraction]] nearby.
