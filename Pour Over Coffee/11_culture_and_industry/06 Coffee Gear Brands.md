# 🛠️ Coffee Gear Brands

Pour over culture runs on **gear**, and a surprisingly small set of brands defines the modern brewer's bench. Some are decades-old manufacturers from Japan and Germany; others are startups born from the [[The Modern Pour Over Renaissance|recent renaissance]]. Together they form an ecosystem where a dripper from one company, a kettle from another, and a scale from a third assemble into a single ritual.

## 🧰 The Core Brands

| Brand | Origin | Known for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hario** | Japan | The [[Hario V60\|V60]], the world's defining conical dripper, plus servers and kettles |
| **Kalita** | Japan | The flat-bottom [[Kalita Wave\|Wave]], favored for forgiving, even extraction |
| **Chemex** | USA | The iconic hourglass [[Chemex\|Chemex]] brewer and its thick bonded filters |
| **Origami** | Japan | The colorful, ribbed [[Origami Dripper\|Origami]] that takes both conical and flat filters |
| **Fellow** | USA | Design-forward kettles (the Stagg EKG) and grinders that defined the modern aesthetic |
| **Comandante** | Germany | The Nitro Blade [[Hand Grinders vs Electric Grinders\|hand grinder]], a benchmark for hand grinding |
| **Acaia** | USA/Taiwan | The Pearl/Lunar Bluetooth brewing [[Scales, Timers, and Servers\|scales]] that made data-driven brewing normal |
| **OXO** | USA | Accessible, well-designed gear including the [[OXO Rapid Brewer\|Rapid Brewer]] |

## 🇯🇵 Heritage vs. Startup

Two cultures meet on the brew bar. The **heritage manufacturers** make the drippers and [[Coffee Filters|filters]] that brewing literally depends on: Hario (founded 1921 as a glass company), Kalita, and Germany's Melitta, whose founder [[Melitta Bentz and the Paper Filter|invented the paper filter]] in 1908. The **renaissance startups** (Fellow, Acaia, Timemore, Weber, and others) entered through design and electronics, turning kettles, scales, and grinders into objects of desire.

> [!note] The filter lock-in
> Many drippers are tied to their maker's filters: a V60 wants a V60 cone, a Kalita wants a Wave filter, a Chemex wants Chemex bonded paper. Brand loyalty in pour over is often really *filter compatibility*. The [[Origami Dripper]] is unusual in accepting both shapes.

## 💡 Why Brands Matter to the Cup

Gear is not neutral. A dripper's [[Conical vs Flat Bottom Drippers|geometry]], its [[Dripper Materials and Heat Retention|material]], a kettle's spout, and a scale's responsiveness all change how a brew behaves. The brands above earned their place because their design choices produce repeatable results. That is why competition brewers at [[The World Brewers Cup]] reach for the same short list.

> [!warning] Gear is not skill
> A 300-dollar kettle and a Bluetooth scale will not fix bad [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind]] or [[Pouring Technique|pour technique]]. The community's recurring lesson: spend first on a good grinder and fresh beans, not on the prettiest dripper.
