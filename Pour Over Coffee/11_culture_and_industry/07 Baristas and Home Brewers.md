# 👩‍🍳 Baristas and Home Brewers

Pour over culture is carried by two overlapping groups of people: the **professional barista** behind the café bar, and the **home brewer** chasing the same cup on a kitchen counter. The remarkable thing about specialty coffee is how porous that line is: professional technique flows freely to amateurs, and obsessive hobbyists sometimes out-nerd the pros.

## 👔 The Professional Craft

A barista is a skilled service professional, not just someone who pushes a button. In a serious café, the role spans espresso, milk, customer education, and increasingly **manual filter brewing** at the [[Coffee Shops and Cafe Culture|pour-over bar]]. Doing it well means mastering [[Dialing In Grind Size|dialing in grind]], reading the [[The Drawdown|drawdown]], managing [[Agitation and Turbulence|agitation]], and tasting critically enough to adjust, all under time pressure, repeatably, drink after drink.

> [!note] Brewing as performance
> The barista's craft peaks at competition. [[The World Brewers Cup]] turns filter brewing into a judged, scripted performance. Many competitors are working baristas refining technique they then teach to everyone else.

## 🏠 The Home-Brewing Community

The home brewer is the engine of the [[The Modern Pour Over Renaissance|renaissance]]. Pour over is cheap to start and deep enough to obsess over, which made it the perfect hobby for a generation that learned from the internet. Home brewers cluster into recognizable types:

| Brewer type | Mindset |
| --- | --- |
| The morning ritualist | Wants one reliable, repeatable cup |
| The tinkerer | Chases variables: grind, water, [[The Brew Ratio\|ratio]], pour |
| The gear collector | Owns more [[Coffee Gear Brands\|drippers]] than they can use |
| The data nerd | Measures [[Refractometers and Measuring Extraction\|extraction]] with a refractometer |

Most brewers drift between these as they learn.

## 🔁 How the Two Groups Feed Each Other

The flow runs both ways. Professionals publish recipes and techniques; home brewers adopt, test, and remix them. Figures who straddle both worlds collapse the distance entirely. Take [[Online Coffee Communities and Content|Lance Hedrick]], a working coffee professional and prolific educator, who teaches café-grade technique to anyone with a [[Hario V60|V60]] and a [[Scales, Timers, and Servers|scale]].

> [!tip] The community's core advice
> Almost every experienced brewer, pro or amateur, gives the same beginner guidance: buy fresh beans, grind right before brewing on a decent [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders|burr grinder]], weigh everything, and change one variable at a time. The craft is less about secret tricks than disciplined repetition.

> [!warning] Gatekeeping and snobbery
> Coffee communities can tip into elitism, dismissing dark roasts, milk drinks, or affordable gear. The healthiest corners reject this: good coffee is whatever the drinker enjoys, brewed with a little care.
