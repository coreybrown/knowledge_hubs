# 🗺️ The Coffee Brewing Control Chart

The **Coffee Brewing Control Chart** is the classic map of brewing space: a graph with **[[Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)|strength (TDS)]]** on the vertical axis and **[[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction yield (EY%)]]** on the horizontal axis. Any brew you make plots as a single dot somewhere on it. A central rectangle marks the historically "ideal" zone, and the regions around it are labelled with how brews drift wrong: too sour, too bitter, too strong, too weak. It is the single most reproduced diagram in coffee science. 📈

## How to Read It

| Region | EY | TDS | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bottom-left | Low | Low | Under-developed & weak |
| Bottom-right | High | Low | Bitter but thin |
| Top-left | Low | High | Sour & strong |
| Top-right | High | High | Bitter & strong |
| **Center box** | ~18–22% | ~1.15–1.45% | The classic "ideal" |

The two axes move semi-independently: slide **left/right** by changing [[Grind Size and Surface Area|grind]], [[The Role of Temperature in Extraction|temperature]], or [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time|time]] (these change extraction); slide **up/down** by changing the [[The Brew Ratio|brew ratio]] (this changes strength). Diagonal lines on the chart represent constant brew ratios, a handy way to see that grinding finer pushes you up *and* to the right at once.

## Origins 🕰️

The chart descends from work by **Dr. Ernest Earl Lockhart** at MIT in the **1950s**, funded by the Coffee Brewing Institute. Lockhart surveyed American drinkers, found a preferred extraction band around **18–22%** and a strength near **1.15–1.35%**, and the result became the **SCA "Golden Cup"** standard. The familiar grid form is often called the **Brewing Control Chart** or, in newer interactive versions, the **"coffee compass."**

## The Critique ⚠️

> [!warning] The "ideal box" is dated and culturally narrow
> Lockhart's data reflected 1950s American palates and the dark, robusta-heavy roasts of the era. Modern light, [[Light Roast and Specialty Coffee|specialty]] roasts frequently taste sweetest at **22–24%** extraction, well outside the original box. Treat the chart as a *coordinate system*, not a verdict.

A second limitation: the chart plots a **single average** EY. It cannot see [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|uneven extraction]], so a brew that lands dead-center can still taste flawed if some grounds over-extracted while others under-extracted. Average is not the same as good.

> [!tip] Use it as a logbook, not a law
> Plot your favourite cups over time. The cluster *you* love is your personal target, and it may sit well outside the printed rectangle.
