# 📏 Grind Size and Surface Area

Grind size is the **master lever** of extraction, and the reason is pure geometry: smaller particles expose vastly more **surface area** to the water, and surface area is where dissolution happens. Halve a particle's diameter and you roughly **double** the surface area for the same mass of coffee, which is why a small twist of the grinder can swing a cup from sour to bitter. This note is the science behind the practical [[Grind Size for Pour Over|grind targets]] in the grinding domain. 🔍

## The Geometry of Extraction

Extraction happens at the **interface** between coffee and water. A whole bean has almost no usable surface relative to its mass; grinding shatters it into thousands of fragments, multiplying the exposed area enormously. More surface means:

- **More dissolution sites**: compounds leave the surface faster.
- **Shorter diffusion paths**: flavor deep inside a particle has less distance to travel out.

Both effects accelerate [[The Science of Extraction|extraction]]. So at a *fixed* time and temperature, **finer grind → higher [[Extraction Yield and Strength|extraction yield]]**, and coarser grind → lower.

| Grind | Surface area | Extraction rate | Flow / drawdown |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coarse | Low | Slow | Fast |
| Medium | Moderate | Balanced | Moderate |
| Fine | High | Fast | Slow (can stall) |

## The Double Effect ⚙️

Grind changes extraction through **two** channels at once, which is why it is so potent:

1. **Surface area**: finer grounds extract faster per unit time.
2. **Flow resistance**: finer grounds pack tighter, slowing [[The Drawdown|drawdown]] and lengthening [[Brew Time and Total Contact Time|contact time]].

Both push extraction the same direction, compounding the effect. At the extreme, an over-fine grind can **stall** the bed entirely, paradoxically risking *over*-extraction from the long contact time. See the practical [[Dialing In Grind Size|dial-in process]].

## Uniformity Matters as Much as Size

> [!warning] Surface area isn't the whole story
> A grinder doesn't make one size; it makes a *distribution*. [[Fines and Boulders|Fines]] have huge surface area and over-extract; boulders have little and under-extract. A wide spread guarantees [[Channeling and Uneven Extraction|uneven extraction]] no matter where you set the dial, which is why a good [[Burr Grinders vs Blade Grinders|burr grinder]] often matters more than the exact setting. See [[Particle Distribution and Uniformity]].

> [!tip] Grind is your single cleanest variable
> When dialing in, hold ratio, water, and pour steady and move grind alone. Sour cup → finer. Bitter, slow, stalling cup → coarser. It is the most direct route onto the [[The Coffee Brewing Control Chart|control chart's]] sweet spot.

## Roast Interacts Too

[[Roast Levels for Pour Over|Darker roasts]] are more brittle and porous, so they grind into more fines and extract faster. That means a dark roast usually wants a **coarser** setting than a light roast at the same target.
