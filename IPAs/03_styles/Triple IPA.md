# Triple IPA

The **Triple IPA** is the extreme upper tier of the IPA family — beers of
10% ABV and beyond, brewed with the heaviest hop charges in craft. It is less a
distinct flavor style than a *strength category*, an escalation of the
[[Double IPA]] into territory where alcohol management becomes the central
brewing problem.

> [!example] Quick stats
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **ABV** | 10.0–13.0%+ |
> | **IBU** | 70–120+ |
> | **Color** | 6–14 SRM (gold to amber) |
> | **Key hops** | Heavy late and dry-hop charges; high-oil American varieties |
> | **Glassware** | Snifter or small tulip (modest pour) |

## A Category of Excess

The Triple IPA emerged as a logical extension of the imperial arms race that
began with the [[Origins of the Double IPA|Double IPA]]. Russian River's Pliny
the Younger is the most famous example. There is no firm boundary with the
Double IPA — most guidelines, including [[BJCP and Style Guidelines]], treat
"triple" informally, simply as a Double IPA pushed past ~10%.

## The Engineering Problem

At this strength the brewer fights physics and biology:

| Challenge | Why it matters | Common solution |
|---|---|---|
| Fermenting to dryness | Yeast struggles in high alcohol | Healthy pitch, stepped sugar additions, [[Fermentation]] management |
| Avoiding hot alcohol | Fusel and solventy notes spike | Controlled temps, simple sugars over crystal malt |
| Hop saturation | Diminishing returns, [[Hop Creep and Refermentation\|hop creep]] | Massive but well-timed [[Dry Hopping]] |
| Drinkability | Beer can turn syrupy | A very lean grain bill — see [[Recipe Formulation]] |

> [!tip] Sugar is the secret
> Triple IPAs lean heavily on simple sugars (dextrose, sucrose). These ferment
> fully, raising ABV without adding body — keeping a 12% beer from drinking like
> a barleywine. See [[Specialty Malts and Adjuncts]].

## Sensory Profile

- **Aroma** — saturated hop intensity; the beer's strength is often barely
  masked.
- **Flavor** — concentrated hop flavor with an unavoidable alcohol presence.
- **Mouthfeel** — full and warming, sometimes viscous; see
  [[The Science of Mouthfeel]].
- **Finish** — dry by design but with lingering heat.

> [!warning] Drink with caution
> A modest pour of a 12% Triple IPA contains as much alcohol as several
> standard beers. These are sipping beers, best shared.
