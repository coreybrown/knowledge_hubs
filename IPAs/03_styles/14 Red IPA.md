# Red IPA

The **Red IPA** is an [[American IPA]] built on an amber-to-red malt base. By
adding crystal and caramel malts to the grain bill, the brewer gives the beer a
deeper color and a layer of caramel, toffee, and toasted flavor — a malt
backbone that the lean [[West Coast IPA]] deliberately avoids. It is the IPA
family's most overtly malt-forward member.

> [!example] Quick stats
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **ABV** | 6.0–8.5% |
> | **IBU** | 50–90 |
> | **Color** | 11–19 SRM (amber to deep red) |
> | **Key hops** | Citrus-pine American — [[Centennial]], [[Cascade]], [[Simcoe]] |
> | **Glassware** | IPA glass or tulip |

## Origins

The Red IPA evolved from the American amber ale — a hoppy amber pushed toward
IPA strength and bitterness. [[BJCP and Style Guidelines]] recognizes it as a
specialty IPA category. It sits at the intersection of two craft staples: the
amber ale and the [[American IPA]].

## The Crystal-Malt Balance

The defining feature is the use of crystal and caramel malts — see
[[Specialty Malts and Adjuncts]]. These contribute:

- **Color** — the characteristic amber-to-garnet hue.
- **Flavor** — caramel, toffee, light toast, dried fruit.
- **Body** — a fuller, rounder mouthfeel than a standard IPA.

> [!warning] The cloying trap
> Crystal malt is unfermentable sugar. Too much leaves the beer sweet, sticky,
> and heavy — the opposite of a refreshing IPA. The art of the Red IPA is using
> just enough caramel character to support, not smother, the hops. See
> [[Recipe Formulation]].

## Hop Pairing

A caramel malt base pairs especially well with **piney, resinous, and dank**
hops, which echo and contrast the toffee sweetness. Bright citrus hops also work
but can read as marmalade against the malt. See [[Hop Oils and Terpenes]].

## Sensory Profile

| Element | Character |
|---|---|
| Appearance | Amber to deep red, usually clear |
| Aroma | Citrus-pine hops over caramel malt |
| Flavor | Hop-forward but with real malt presence |
| Bitterness | Firm, balanced against caramel sweetness |
| Mouthfeel | Medium to full — see [[The Science of Mouthfeel]] |

## Place in the Family

The Red IPA is a *malt-forward* IPA, a sibling to the [[Rye IPA]] and the
[[Black IPA]] — styles that depart from the standard lean grist.
