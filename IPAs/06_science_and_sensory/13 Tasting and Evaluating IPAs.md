# Tasting and Evaluating IPAs

To *evaluate* an IPA is to taste it deliberately and systematically, assessing it
against a standard rather than simply enjoying it. This note describes a structured
method used by judges, brewers, and serious enthusiasts, built on the vocabulary of
the [[Beer Flavor Wheel]].

## Tasting vs. Drinking

> [!note] A different mode of attention
> [[How to Taste an IPA]] introduces tasting for pleasure. *Evaluation* adds rigor:
> a fixed sequence, a vocabulary, an awareness of bias, and a judgment against style
> expectations like those in [[BJCP and Style Guidelines]].

## The Evaluation Sequence

Always assess in the same order, appearance, then aroma, then flavor, then
mouthfeel, then overall, so nothing is skipped.

| Step | What to assess | IPA-specific notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Appearance** | Color, clarity, head | Brilliant clarity ([[West Coast IPA]]) vs. stable haze ([[New England IPA]]) |
| **Aroma** | Intensity, descriptors, faults | Citrus, pine, tropical (see [[Hop Aroma Compounds]]) |
| **Flavor** | Malt/hop balance, bitterness, finish | Where [[IBU and Perceived Bitterness|perceived bitterness]] is judged |
| **Mouthfeel** | Body, carbonation, astringency | The "pillowy" axis ([[The Science of Mouthfeel]]) |
| **Overall** | Balance, drinkability, faults | Does it match its style? |

## Aroma: Get It Right First

Aroma is the most fragile and most informative dimension. Swirl gently to release
volatiles, then take **short sniffs**. Use both **orthonasal** smell (through the
nose) and, while drinking, **retronasal** smell (aroma rising from the throat);
the latter carries much of perceived "flavor."

> [!tip] Smell before the aroma fades
> [[Thiols and Hop Burst|Thiols]] and light terpenes dissipate fast in the glass.
> Evaluate aroma within the first minute or two.

## Controlling for Bias

Rigorous evaluation actively fights perceptual bias:

- **Temperature**: serve at the correct [[Serving Temperature]]; cold mutes aroma
  and flattens faults.
- **Glassware**: a consistent, aroma-concentrating glass; see [[Glassware for IPAs]].
- **Order effects**: palate fatigue builds; rotate samples, rinse, rest.
- **Expectation bias**: label, brewery reputation, and hype distort judgment.
  Serious evaluation is done **blind**.
- **Freshness**: note the [[Reading an IPA Label|packaged-on date]]; staleness
  (see [[Hop Fade and Oxidation]]) is not the recipe's fault.

> [!example] A structured flight
> Tasting four West Coast IPAs blind, in identical glasses, at 8 °C, scoring each on
> the same sheet, that is evaluation. Building such a lineup is covered in
> [[Building an IPA Tasting Flight]].

## Scoring Against a Standard

Formal evaluation ends in a judgment: does the beer meet its **style**? Compare
against [[BJCP and Style Guidelines]] and the [[IPA Style Comparison Table]]. A
technically flawless beer can still score poorly if it does not represent its
declared style.
