# Off-Flavors in IPA

An **off-flavor** is a flavor or aroma that does not belong — a sign of a process
error, contamination, or staling. Hop-forward beers are not immune; in fact some
faults are *easier* to spot in an IPA, while others hide behind the hops. Learning
to name and diagnose them is core to [[Sensory Training and Panels|quality control]].

## The Major Off-Flavors

> [!warning] Faults vs. style
> Some "off-flavors" are appropriate in certain styles (mild diacetyl in some
> British ales, clove phenol in a [[Belgian IPA]]). Context decides whether
> something is a fault.

| Off-flavor | Tastes/smells like | Main cause | Fix / prevention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diacetyl** | Butter, butterscotch, slick | Incomplete diacetyl rest; [[Dry Hop Creep Explained|hop creep]]; infection | Allow a diacetyl rest; finish fermentation before packaging |
| **DMS** | Cooked corn, canned vegetable | Short/weak boil; slow wort cooling | Vigorous 60-min+ boil; fast chill — see [[The Boil]] |
| **Acetaldehyde** | Green apple, raw pumpkin | Yeast pulled early; immature beer | Give full fermentation + conditioning time |
| **Oxidation** | Wet cardboard, sherry, papery | Oxygen pickup post-fermentation | Low-oxygen transfer/packaging — see [[Hop Fade and Oxidation]] |
| **Astringency** | Drying, puckering, harsh | Over-sparging, high pH, hot/over-extracted hops | Control mash pH; gentle [[Lautering and Sparging]] |
| **Light-struck** | Skunk, burnt rubber | UV light on iso-alpha acids | Cans/brown glass; avoid light — see [[Light-Struck Beer and Skunking]] |
| **Phenolic** | Clove, plastic, medicinal | Wild yeast; chlorinated water | Sanitation; use [[Water Treatment for Brewing|treated water]] |
| **Acetic / sour** | Vinegar, tart (unintended) | Bacterial contamination | Sanitation; oxygen control |

## Faults That Especially Plague IPAs

### Oxidation — the hop killer

Because an IPA's value *is* its hop aroma, [[Hop Fade and Oxidation|oxidation]] is
the most damaging IPA-specific fault. A heavily dry-hopped, polyphenol-rich
[[New England IPA]] stales fastest of all — see
[[Hop Haze and Colloidal Stability]].

### Diacetyl from hop creep

[[Dry Hopping]] can restart fermentation ([[Dry Hop Creep Explained|hop creep]]),
and renewed yeast activity throws **diacetyl**. A beer that tasted clean at
packaging can turn buttery weeks later.

### Astringency masquerading as bitterness

Harsh, drying astringency is easy to confuse with hop [[The Chemistry of Hop Bitterness|bitterness]].
True iso-alpha-acid bitterness is clean; astringency *rasps*. The distinction
matters for [[The Science of Mouthfeel|mouthfeel]] and water chemistry.

> [!tip] Diagnosis is a sensory skill
> Brewers train panels with **spiked samples** — beer dosed with a known fault
> compound — so the team recognizes a defect at threshold. Pair this note with the
> [[Beer Flavor Wheel]] and [[Sensory Training and Panels]].
