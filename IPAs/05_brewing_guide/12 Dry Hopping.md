# Dry Hopping

**Dry hopping** is the addition of hops to beer *after* the boil, during or after [[Fermentation]], with no heat involved. Because there is no isomerization, dry hopping contributes **aroma and flavor with virtually no bitterness**. It is the single most important technique for the aromatic punch of the modern IPA.

## Why It Works

Boiling destroys the most volatile [[Hop Oils and Terpenes|hop oils]]. Dry hopping skips heat entirely, so delicate aromatics (citrus, tropical, floral, dank) dissolve into the beer and survive into the glass. When dry hops meet active yeast, [[Biotransformation]] generates entirely new compounds, including bright [[Thiols and Hop Burst|thiols]].

## When to Dry Hop

| Timing | Effect |
|---|---|
| Active fermentation (day 2–4) | Strong biotransformation, juicy character |
| End of fermentation | Cleaner, more "true to variety" aroma |
| Cold / post-crash | Maximum retention, minimal scrubbing |

> [!note] CO2 scrubbing
> Dry hopping during active fermentation has a cost: escaping CO2 strips out some aroma, called "scrubbing." Many brewers split the charge: one during fermentation for biotransformation, one cold for retention.

## How Much and How Long

- **Rate**: 4–12 g/L for a standard IPA; far more in a [[Double Dry Hopping|double-dry-hopped]] beer.
- **Contact time**: **24–72 hours** is the sweet spot. Longer extracts grassy, vegetal, astringent character.

> [!warning] Don't over-steep
> Beyond ~3–4 days, dry hops stop adding pleasant aroma and start adding harsh polyphenols and a grassy "hop tea" note. See [[Off-Flavors in IPA]].

## Technique

> [!tip] Best practices
> - **Minimize oxygen**: dry hops carry air; purge the vessel with CO2. See [[Carbonation and Packaging]].
> - **Maximize contact**: pellets dissolve faster than whole cones; gentle rousing helps.
> - **Keep it cool-ish**: warm dry hopping extracts faster but also extracts more harshness.
> - **Watch for hop creep**: dry hops carry enzymes that can restart fermentation; see [[Hop Creep and Refermentation]].

## Dry Hopping by Style

A [[West Coast IPA]] uses a moderate, clean dry hop for definition; a [[New England IPA]] uses a massive multi-stage charge for saturated aroma. Heavy or multiple charges are covered in [[Double Dry Hopping]].

> [!warning] Hop fade
> Dry-hop aroma is fragile and fades within weeks of packaging. Drink IPAs fresh. See [[Hop Fade and Oxidation]] and [[IPA Freshness and Shelf Life]].
