# The IPA Brewing Process Overview

Brewing an IPA is the controlled conversion of starch into sugar, sugar into alcohol, and raw hops into aroma. This note maps the full **grain-to-glass** journey so the detailed technique notes have a frame to hang on.

## The Sequence

> [!example] A typical brew day and timeline
> 1. **Mill** the grain — [[Milling and the Grain Bill]]
> 2. **Mash** at ~63–67 °C for 60 min — [[Mashing]]
> 3. **Lauter and sparge** to collect wort — [[Lautering and Sparging]]
> 4. **Boil** 60 min with hop additions — [[The Boil]]
> 5. **Whirlpool** with late hops — [[Whirlpool and Hop Stand]]
> 6. **Chill** to pitching temperature
> 7. **Pitch yeast and ferment** ~5–7 days — [[Fermentation]]
> 8. **Dry hop** during or after fermentation — [[Dry Hopping]]
> 9. **Package and carbonate** — [[Carbonation and Packaging]]

Steps 1–6 are **brew day** (4–6 hours). Steps 7–9 span **two to four weeks**.

## Hot Side vs. Cold Side

Brewers divide the process at the chiller.

| Side | Stages | Dominant concern |
|---|---|---|
| Hot side | Mill → whirlpool | Extraction, bitterness, sanitation by heat |
| Cold side | Chill → package | Sanitation by cleanliness, oxygen control |

The cold side is where IPAs are won or lost. After the boil there is no heat to kill microbes, and oxygen — harmless on the hot side — becomes the enemy that triggers [[Hop Fade and Oxidation]].

## What Makes IPA Brewing Distinctive

A pale ale and an IPA follow the same skeleton, but the IPA leans hard on **hops** and demands extra discipline:

1. **Massive hop loads** — modern IPAs may use 1–3+ kg of hops per barrel, mostly late.
2. **Cold-side hopping** — [[Whirlpool and Hop Stand]] and [[Dry Hopping]] add aroma without harsh bitterness.
3. **Oxygen paranoia** — hop aromatics oxidize fast; see [[Carbonation and Packaging]].
4. **Hop creep awareness** — dry hops carry enzymes that can restart fermentation, covered in [[Hop Creep and Refermentation]].

> [!warning] Freshness is a feature
> Unlike a stout or a lager, an IPA degrades quickly. The process should be optimized for speed to package and minimal oxygen pickup. See [[IPA Freshness and Shelf Life]].
