# Best Session IPAs

A [[Session IPA]] delivers full IPA aroma and bitterness at a strength low enough to
enjoy more than one, typically 3.5% to 5% ABV. The hard part is keeping flavor when
you remove the alcohol and malt that carry it. These picks manage it.

> [!note] The session test: would you have a second?
> The whole point of the style is drinkability. Picks below are judged not just on a
> single sip but on whether they stay satisfying across an afternoon. See [[How These Were Selected]] and [[Best Time and Occasion to Drink an IPA]].

## The picks

| Beer | Brewery | Location | ABV | Why it's notable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All Day IPA | Founders | Grand Rapids, MI | ~4.7% | The style's standard-bearer; balanced, hugely available |
| DayTime | Lagunitas | Petaluma, CA | ~4.0% | Crisp, citrusy, genuinely thirst-quenching |
| Easy Jack | Firestone Walker | Paso Robles, CA | ~4.5% | International hop blend; aromatic well beyond its strength |
| Go To IPA | Stone | Escondido, CA | ~4.5% | "Hop bursting" packs aroma into a light frame |

| Lil Heaven | Two Roads | Stratford, CT | ~4.8% | Tropical, juicy, easy-drinking East Coast session |

## What links these beers

A good session IPA fights one core problem: a thin malt base gives hops nowhere to
sit, exposing every flaw. Brewers compensate with **aroma-focused hopping** (heavy
[[Whirlpool and Hop Stand]] and [[Dry Hopping]] charges rather than hot-side
bittering) plus small amounts of body-building malt or wheat for mouthfeel. See [[The Science of Mouthfeel]]. Because alcohol no longer masks defects, clean [[Fermentation]]
and freedom from [[Off-Flavors in IPA]] matter even more than usual.

> [!tip] Session IPA vs. pale ale
> The line between a session IPA and a hoppy [[American IPA|pale ale]] is blurry and
> mostly a marketing choice. Judge the beer in the glass, not the word on the can;
> see [[Reading an IPA Label]].

> [!example] Why the style exists
> The session IPA rose as a deliberate answer to the [[Rise of the West Coast IPA| arms race]] of ever-bigger, boozier beers, proof that [[Modern IPA Diversification]]
> sometimes means dialing *down*.

## Honorable mentions

Oskar Blues Pinner, Sierra Nevada's lighter offerings, and many local session IPAs
shine when fresh. Because the style is light and inexpensive to ship, several picks
here also qualify as [[Best Budget IPAs]].
