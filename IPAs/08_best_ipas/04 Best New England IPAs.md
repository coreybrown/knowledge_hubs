# Best New England IPAs

Hazy, soft, juicy, and low in perceived bitterness, the [[New England IPA]]
rewrote what an IPA could be. The beers below are the critic's picks: the ones that
either defined the style or execute it with rare polish.

> [!note] The freshness problem is acute here
> [[New England IPA]] is the most perishable IPA style. Its aromatics collapse and its
> haze can dull within weeks. Every pick below assumes you drink it **fresh**, ideally
> within a month of packaging. See [[IPA Freshness and Shelf Life]].

## The picks

| Beer | Brewery | Location | ABV | Why it's notable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Heady Topper | The Alchemist | Stowe, VT | ~8.0% | The beer that launched the style; a hazy [[Double IPA]] icon |
| Julius | Tree House | Charlton, MA | ~6.8% | Benchmark juicy [[New England IPA]]: peach, mango, soft body |
| Sap / Green | Tree House | Charlton, MA | ~7.5%+ | Among the most lauded hazy DIPAs anywhere |
| Trillium offerings | Trillium | Boston, MA | ~6–8% | A rotating range of consistently elite hazy IPAs |
| Focal Banger | The Alchemist | Stowe, VT | ~7.0% | [[Citra]]/[[Mosaic]] showcase; more sessionable than Heady |
| Substance | Bissell Brothers | Portland, ME | ~6.6% | Defining Maine hazy, pulpy and citrus-forward |

## What links these beers

The New England style trades clarity and bitterness for **aroma and texture**. These
beers lean on huge late and dry-hop charges of [[Citra]], [[Mosaic]], and [[Galaxy]],
soft water with a low [[Water Chemistry and the Sulfate-Chloride Ratio|sulfate]] level,
oat- and wheat-laden grists, and fruity [[IPA Yeast Strains|yeast]] that drives
[[Biotransformation]]. The result is a pillowy mouthfeel and a "juice" character far
removed from the [[West Coast IPA]]. The story of how this happened is told in [[The New England IPA Emergence]].

> [!tip] Hype is part of the experience, and part of the problem
> Many of these beers sell out within hours and travel poorly. See [[The Hype Beer Phenomenon]]. A fresh local hazy IPA will often beat a famous one bought secondhand
> and stale.

> [!example] Haze is not flavor
> Cloudiness alone signals nothing about quality. See [[Hop Haze and Colloidal Stability]] and [[Clarification and Haze Management]]. The best New England IPAs are
> hazy *and* deeply aromatic; the worst are merely murky.

## Honorable mentions

Other Bissell Brothers, Other Half (NY), Hill Farmstead (VT), Foam (VT), and Equilibrium
(NY) all field excellent hazy IPAs. The style now spans the globe. See [[Globalization of IPA]] and [[Modern IPA Diversification]].
