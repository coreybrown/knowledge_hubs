# Best Budget IPAs

The most coveted IPAs are often expensive, rare, and gone in hours. This note pushes
in the opposite direction: excellent IPAs that are **affordable, widely distributed,
and reliably fresh**. For everyday drinking, these frequently beat the hype beers.

> [!note] Value is a real criterion
> Per [[How These Were Selected]], accessibility is weighted heavily here. A great beer
> you can buy at a normal store, fresh, for a fair price often delivers more genuine
> enjoyment than a famous beer bought stale and secondhand.

## The picks

| Beer | Brewery | Location | ABV | Why it's notable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Torpedo Extra IPA | Sierra Nevada | Chico, CA | ~7.2% | A modern classic; nationally available and very fresh |
| Two-Hearted Ale | Bell's | Comstock, MI | ~7.0% | Consistently top-rated yet sensibly priced |
| All Day IPA | Founders | Grand Rapids, MI | ~4.7% | A [[Session IPA]] sold in big, cheap variety packs |
| 60 Minute IPA | Dogfish Head | Milton, DE | ~6.0% | Continuously-hopped; an everyday workhorse |
| Voodoo Ranger IPA | New Belgium | Fort Collins, CO | ~7.0% | A whole value-priced IPA range with wide reach |
| Daisy Cutter | Half Acre | Chicago, IL | ~5.2% | Critically adored pale ale at an everyday price |

## Why these are good value

The economics of [[Beer Distribution and the Three-Tier System|distribution]] reward
scale: large regional breweries can sell genuinely excellent beer cheaply because
volume spreads fixed costs. Crucially, wide distribution also means **high turnover** —
cans sell quickly and reach you fresh, which matters enormously given [[Hop Fade and Oxidation]]. A cheap, fresh IPA usually outperforms an expensive, old one.

> [!tip] Where the real savings hide
> The cheapest path to good IPA is often your **own local brewery**: no shipping cost,
> maximum freshness, and beer brewed for your market. See [[IPA in Bars and Taprooms]]
> and [[Regional Drinking Cultures]].

> [!example] Price ≠ quality
> Several beers on this list also appear among the [[Iconic IPAs That Defined the Style]]. The [[The Hype Beer Phenomenon|hype economy]] inflates prices for scarcity
> and packaging, not for what is in the glass — see [[Untappd and Beer Rating Culture]].

## How to shop smart

Buy from a store with fast turnover, check the [[Reading an IPA Label|packaged-on date]], favor cans over clear bottles to avoid [[Light-Struck Beer and Skunking]], and
prefer the freshest beer over the most famous. The [[Best Session IPAs]] list overlaps
heavily with budget value.
