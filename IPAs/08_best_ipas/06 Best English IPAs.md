# Best English IPAs

The [[English IPA]] is the style's ancestor, and a living style in its own right.
Where American IPAs shout, the English IPA converses: earthy, floral hops, a real
biscuit-and-toffee malt presence, and a bitterness that supports rather than dominates.
The picks below honor that restraint.

> [!note] A quieter style, judged on its own terms
> Do not measure these against a [[West Coast IPA]]. They aim for balance, not
> intensity. Per [[How These Were Selected]], each is judged against what the English
> style sets out to do.

## The picks

| Beer | Brewery | Location | ABV | Why it's notable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jaipur | Thornbridge | Bakewell, England | ~5.9% | Modern classic; bridges English balance and American aroma |
| Worthington's White Shield | Molson Coors (Burton) | Burton-on-Trent, England | ~5.6% | Direct descendant of Victorian Burton pale ale |
| Meantime India Pale Ale | Meantime | London, England | ~7.4% | A studied revival of the strong historical IPA |
| India Pale Ale | Fuller's | London, England | ~5.3% | Honeyed, marmalade-tinged traditional English IPA |
| Punk IPA | BrewDog | Ellon, Scotland | ~5.4% | The beer that brought modern hop-forward IPA to UK shelves |
| IPA | Greene King | Bury St Edmunds, England | ~3.6% | A true cask "ordinary bitter"-strength IPA; living history |

> [!example] How old is "English IPA"?
> White Shield traces a continuous line back to the great pale ales of [[Burton-on-Trent and Burton Pale Ale|Burton-on-Trent]]. Greene King IPA, at 3.6%, shows how far the
> *name* drifted in Britain after the [[Decline of IPA in Britain]]: a low-strength
> bitter that kept the historic label.

## What links these beers

English IPAs are built on English ingredients and English water. Expect [[Noble and English Hops]] (Fuggle, East Kent Goldings, Challenger) giving earthy, floral, marmalade
notes; richer [[Specialty Malts and Adjuncts|crystal malt]] character; and the famously
sulfate-rich [[Water Chemistry and the Sulfate-Chloride Ratio|Burton water]] that gives
a crisp, dry "Burton snatch." Their lineage runs through [[Hodgson and the East India Trade]] and [[IPA in the British Empire]].

> [!tip] Seek it on cask
> Many English IPAs are at their best as cask ale: gently carbonated and cellar-cool.
> See [[Draft vs Can vs Bottle]] and [[Regional Drinking Cultures]].

## Honorable mentions

Marston's Old Empire, Wye Valley HPA, and various Burton-brewed pale ales keep the
tradition alive. Modern UK breweries increasingly blur the line with [[American IPA]]
hopping; see [[Modern IPA Diversification]].
