# Best Black and Specialty IPAs

Beyond the mainstream styles lies a more adventurous corner of the IPA family: dark,
spiced, soured, fruited, and fermented-differently beers that take the hop-forward
template somewhere unexpected. This note collects critic's picks for the [[Black IPA]]
and the broader world of [[Specialty and Experimental IPAs]].

> [!note] Novelty alone is not merit
> A weird beer is not automatically a good one. Per [[How These Were Selected]], these
> picks earn their place by being well-made *and* distinctive — the twist has to
> actually improve the drink.

## Black IPAs

The [[Black IPA]] — also called Cascadian Dark Ale — pairs IPA hopping with dark malt
that contributes color and a light roast without heavy bitterness.

| Beer | Brewery | Location | ABV | Why it's notable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sublimely Self-Righteous | Stone | Escondido, CA | ~8.7% | An early, influential strong black IPA |
| Wookey Jack | Firestone Walker | Paso Robles, CA | ~8.3% | A [[Rye IPA]]/black IPA hybrid; peppery and resinous |
| Back in Black | 21st Amendment | San Leandro, CA | ~6.8% | A clean, widely available reference black IPA |

> [!example] A category that fell out of fashion
> The black IPA peaked around 2011 and has since faded. Critics still rate the best
> examples highly — proof that [[Modern IPA Diversification]] produces styles that
> *deserve* survival even when fashion moves on.

## What links these beers

Specialty IPAs change one variable while keeping the hop focus. A [[Black IPA]] adds
dehusked dark [[Specialty Malts and Adjuncts|malt]]; a [[Rye IPA]] adds spicy rye; a
[[Red IPA]] adds caramel malt; a [[White IPA]], [[Brett IPA]], or [[Belgian IPA]]
changes the [[IPA Yeast Strains|yeast]]; a [[Milkshake IPA]] or [[Sour IPA]] alters
body or acidity; a [[Brut IPA]] or [[Cold IPA]] reengineers fermentation. Each is a
controlled experiment in what "IPA" can mean.

> [!tip] Read the label closely
> Specialty IPAs vary wildly. [[Reading an IPA Label]] and a quick check of the
> [[IPA Style Comparison Table]] will tell you what you're about to drink.
