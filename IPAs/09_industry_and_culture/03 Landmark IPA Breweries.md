# Landmark IPA Breweries

A handful of breweries did not merely sell IPA — they **defined eras of it**. Each landmark brewery is attached to a turning point in the style's evolution, and tracing them is a tour through the IPA's history.

> [!note] Breweries, not just beers
> The [[Best IPAs|best beers]] domain ranks individual products. This note ranks *institutions* — breweries whose influence outlived any single release.

## The Founders of Modern IPA

| Brewery | Era / Contribution |
|---|---|
| Anchor Brewing | [[Anchor Liberty Ale and the First Modern IPA\|Liberty Ale]] (1975) — the dry-hopped ancestor of American IPA |
| Sierra Nevada | Sierra Nevada Pale Ale and later Torpedo — popularized [[Cascade]] hops and the [[The American Craft Beer Revolution\|craft template]] |
| Bert Grant's | Brewed what is widely cited as the first modern American IPA |

## The West Coast Architects

The [[Rise of the West Coast IPA|West Coast style]] was built by breweries that pushed bitterness and hop intensity:

- **Stone Brewing** — Stone IPA and Ruination made aggressive bitterness a badge of pride.
- **Russian River** — Pliny the Elder and Pliny the Younger set the standard for the [[Double IPA]] and [[Triple IPA]].
- **Ballast Point, AleSmith, Green Flash** — San Diego's deep bench of [[West Coast IPA]] specialists.
- **Lagunitas** — scaled hop-forward beer to national distribution.

> [!example] The Pliny effect
> Russian River's Pliny the Elder is so influential that it functions as a reference standard — see [[Origins of the Double IPA]] and [[Iconic IPAs That Defined the Style]].

## The New England Pioneers

The [[The New England IPA Emergence|hazy revolution]] traces to a small group of East Coast breweries:

- **The Alchemist** — Heady Topper made the soft, hazy [[New England IPA]] a cultural event.
- **Tree House Brewing** — perfected the juicy, pillowy expression and the [[The Hype Beer Phenomenon|allocation model]].
- **Hill Farmstead, Trillium, Other Half** — extended the style and the hype culture around it.

## The Modern and Global Names

The IPA's [[Globalization of IPA|globalization]] produced influential breweries far from California:

- **BrewDog** (Scotland) — Punk IPA carried the style across Europe.
- **Cloudwater** (England) — helped legitimize the hazy IPA in the UK and pioneered rapid-rotation releases.
- **Garage Beer, Verdant, Omnipollo** — European haze and [[Milkshake IPA|novelty]] specialists.

> [!tip] How to read this list
> Influence is not the same as size. A tiny brewery like The Alchemist reshaped global brewing with one beer, while a large one may simply distribute widely. Both kinds of impact matter.
