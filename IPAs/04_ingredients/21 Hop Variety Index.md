# Hop Variety Index

This is the quick-reference catalog of the major [[Hops|hop]] varieties used in [[What Is an IPA|IPA]] brewing. Each row links to a full profile; use the descriptors and alpha acid figures to scan for a fit, then read the dedicated note for detail.

## Modern American Hops

| Variety | Alpha % | Origin | Released | Signature character |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|----------------------|
| [[Citra]] | ~11–15% | USA | 2008 | Grapefruit, lime, passionfruit, lychee |
| [[Mosaic]] | ~11–14% | USA | 2012 | Blueberry, mango, pine, dank |
| [[Cascade]] | ~4.5–7% | USA | 1972 | Grapefruit, floral; the craft pioneer |
| [[Centennial]] | ~8–11.5% | USA | 1990 | Citrus, floral, "super Cascade" |
| [[Simcoe]] | ~12–14% | USA | 2000 | Pine, dank, grapefruit, passionfruit |
| [[Amarillo]] | ~8–11% | USA | ~2000 | Orange, floral, melon |
| [[Sabro]] | ~12–16% | USA | 2018 | Coconut, tangerine, cedar |
| [[Strata]] | ~11–13% | USA | 2018 | Passionfruit, strawberry, dank |

## How to Use This Index

> [!tip] Reading the alpha column
> Alpha acid % indicates **bittering potential**, not aroma quality. Low-alpha noble hops are aroma treasures; high-alpha figures simply mean efficient kettle bittering. For aroma, read the [[Hop Oils and Terpenes|oil-driven]] descriptors instead.

> [!note] Choosing a hop
> Group decisions by **role and style**. A classic [[West Coast IPA]] leans on Simcoe, Centennial, and Amarillo; a [[New England IPA]] leans on Citra, Mosaic, and Galaxy; an [[English IPA]] leans on the traditional European set. Most great recipes **blend** across categories.

> [!warning] Availability is not guaranteed
> Many of the best modern hops (Citra, Mosaic, Simcoe, Galaxy, Nelson Sauvin) are **proprietary and contracted**. Inclusion here does not mean a brewer can simply buy them; see [[Hop Contracts and the Hop Supply Chain]].
