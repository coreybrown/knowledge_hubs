# Yeast

**Yeast** is the living ingredient — the microorganism that turns sweet wort into beer.
It is the most quietly powerful of the four [[Ingredients|pillars]]: invisible in the
glass, yet decisive in how an [[What Is an IPA|IPA]] tastes, finishes, and even smells
of [[Hops]].

## What Yeast Is

Brewing yeast is a single-celled fungus, almost always a strain of *Saccharomyces*.
During [[Fermentation]] it consumes the fermentable sugars supplied by [[Malt]] and
excretes two primary products — **ethanol** and **carbon dioxide** — plus a host of
trace flavor compounds.

| Product | Sensory result |
|---------|----------------|
| Ethanol | Alcohol, warmth, body |
| Carbon dioxide | Carbonation, foam |
| Esters | Fruity notes (banana, pear, stone fruit) |
| Phenols | Spicy, clove, peppery notes (notable in [[Belgian IPA]]) |
| Fusel alcohols | Solventy "hot" notes if fermentation is mishandled |

## Ale vs Lager Yeast

The fundamental split is between **ale** and **lager** yeast:

- **Ale yeast** (*S. cerevisiae*) — ferments warm (~18–22 °C), fast, often "top-cropping,"
  and produces expressive esters. The yeast of nearly every IPA.
- **Lager yeast** (*S. pastorianus*) — ferments cold (~8–13 °C), slow, and very clean,
  giving the crisp neutrality behind the [[Cold IPA]] and [[India Pale Lager]].

## Why Yeast Matters to an IPA

> [!note] Yeast shapes hop expression
> Yeast does more than make alcohol. Through [[Biotransformation]] it cleaves bound
> hop glycosides and converts oils such as geraniol — actively *changing* hop aroma.
> A clean strain lets hops speak literally; an expressive strain can amplify or
> recolor them. The same hop bill ferments into a different beer on a different yeast.

**Attenuation** — the share of sugar a strain ferments — sets how dry or full the
beer finishes. A highly attenuative strain dries out a [[Double IPA]] into dangerous
drinkability; a lower-attenuating strain leaves the rounder body of a
[[New England IPA]].

> [!tip] Pitch healthy, pitch enough
> Off-flavors in IPA — solventy fusels, harsh phenols, diacetyl — usually trace to
> stressed yeast: underpitching, poor aeration, or wrong temperature. Healthy yeast
> management is the cheapest quality upgrade a brewer has. See [[Off-Flavors in IPA]].

The specific strains that brewers reach for — Chico, London III, kveik, and lager
strains — are catalogued in [[IPA Yeast Strains]].
