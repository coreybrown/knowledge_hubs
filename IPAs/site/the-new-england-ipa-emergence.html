📜History

The New England IPA Emergence

2 min read·505 words
historynew-england-ipahazy

By the early 2010s, the IPA had spent two decades chasing bitterness. Then a handful of brewers in the northeastern United States flipped the formula entirely. The New England IPA (NEIPA), soft and hazy and bursting with juicy aroma, became the defining beer of the 2010s.

#A Deliberate Reaction

The NEIPA was, in part, a rebellion against the West Coast IPA. Where the West Coast style prized aggressive bitterness and crystal clarity, the New England style prized the opposite:

TraitWest Coast IPANew England IPA
BitternessHigh, lingeringLow, soft
AppearanceBrilliantly clearOpaque, "hazy" / turbid
MouthfeelCrisp, dryPillowy, full, juicy
Hop expressionPine, resin, citrus rindTropical fruit, juice, "hop burst"
WaterHigh sulfateHigh chloride (see Water Chemistry and the Sulfate-Chloride Ratio)

#The Founding Breweries

Three breweries are central to the origin story:

  • The Alchemist (Vermont): John Kimmich's Heady Topper, a Double IPA canned from 2011, is the foundational NEIPA. Famously instructed to be drunk straight from the can, it became the style's first cult object.
  • Hill Farmstead (Vermont): Shaun Hill's acclaimed, intensely aromatic hoppy ales reinforced the regional reputation.
  • Tree House Brewing (Massachusetts): turned the hazy IPA into a national pilgrimage destination.
  • Trillium Brewing (Massachusetts): helped define and commercialise the soft, juicy profile.
ℹVermont and Massachusetts as the cradle

The style is named for its birthplace. New England's brewers, drinkers, and a wave of hype-driven demand co-created it within a few intense years.

#How the Haze Happens

The signature turbidity is no accident. It comes from a combination of techniques covered across the brewing domain:

  • High-protein grists using oats and wheat (see Specialty Malts and Adjuncts).
  • Fruity, low-flocculating English-style yeast.
  • Massive late and dry-hop additions, often double dry-hopped, driving Biotransformation of hop compounds.
  • Chloride-forward water for a fuller, rounder mouthfeel.

The haze itself is a colloidal suspension of proteins, polyphenols, and hop material. Whether haze causes flavor or merely accompanies it is debated, but the soft, juicy character is real and is driven heavily by thiols and hop aroma compounds.

▲The fragility trade-off

NEIPAs are gorgeous fresh and fade fast. Their low bitterness and high hop-oil load make them especially vulnerable to oxidation. Freshness is non-negotiable; see IPA Freshness and Shelf Life.

#A Permanent Branch

The NEIPA did not replace the West Coast IPA; it split the family tree. Today both coexist as major branches of the IPA Family Tree, and the hazy style spawned its own descendants, most notably the dessert-like Milkshake IPA. It also drove the sub-style explosion of the late 2010s.
