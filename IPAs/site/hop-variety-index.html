2 min read·367 words

This is the quick-reference catalog of the major hop varieties used in IPA brewing. Each row links to a full profile; use the descriptors and alpha acid figures to scan for a fit, then read the dedicated note for detail.

#How to Use This Index

✦Reading the alpha column

Alpha acid % indicates bittering potential, not aroma quality. Low-alpha noble hops are aroma treasures; high-alpha figures simply mean efficient kettle bittering. For aroma, read the oil-driven descriptors instead.

ℹChoosing a hop

Group decisions by role and style. A classic West Coast IPA leans on Simcoe, Centennial, and Amarillo; a New England IPA leans on Citra, Mosaic, and Galaxy; an English IPA leans on the traditional European set. Most great recipes blend across categories.

▲Availability is not guaranteed

Many of the best modern hops — Citra, Mosaic, Simcoe, Galaxy, Nelson Sauvin — are proprietary and contracted. Inclusion here does not mean a brewer can simply buy them; see Hop Contracts and the Hop Supply Chain.
