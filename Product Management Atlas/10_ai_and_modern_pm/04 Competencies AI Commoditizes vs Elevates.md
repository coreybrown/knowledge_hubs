# Competencies AI Commoditizes vs Elevates

Let me make this concrete by mapping it onto the actual twelve competencies of the Atlas. Not "AI changes everything" — *which* parts, in *which* direction. Reforge framed the strategic move as **"moving to higher ground"**: as the mechanical work floods, you climb to the work that doesn't. This note is the map of where higher ground is.

## The map

> [!note] The dividing line
> AI commoditizes the **production of artifacts** and the **retrieval of information**. It elevates **judgment, taste, framing, and influence** — the things that decide whether the artifact was worth producing.

| Competency | What's getting cheaper | What's getting more valuable |
|---|---|---|
| [[Feature Specification]] | Drafting the PRD; formatting; first-pass user stories | Choosing the outcome; deciding what *not* to build |
| [[Product Delivery]] | Status reports, ticket grooming, coordination overhead | Trade-off calls; explaining the "why"; unblocking |
| [[Product Quality]] | Surfacing bugs and triage suggestions | Defining what "good" means — now via [[Writing Evals for AI Products\|evals]] |
| [[Fluency with Data]] | Querying, charting, first-pass analysis | Causal reasoning; asking the right question; acting |
| [[Voice of the Customer]] | Synthesizing feedback across thousands of inputs | Knowing which signal is real and what to do about it |
| [[User Experience Design]] | Generating UI variations and flows in seconds | Taste; coherence; designing for failure modes |
| [[Strategic Impact]] | First-pass market and competitive scans | The actual strategic bet and its sequencing |
| [[Product Vision and Roadmapping]] | Roadmap drafts, formatting, options generation | Vision; saying no; the burden of choice |
| [[Stakeholder Management]] | Drafting updates, meeting notes, recaps | Trust, persuasion, conflict resolution |

The left column is converging toward zero marginal cost. The right column is appreciating.

## Why the premium on judgment is rising

Reforge put it plainly: "the premium on judgment and taste has never been higher." And the reason is the counterintuitive twist — **AI doesn't reduce complexity, it multiplies it.** When you can generate ten roadmaps, twenty prototypes, and fifty spec variants in an afternoon, the scarce skill isn't generation. It's *choosing*. The burden of choice lands on the PM, and choosing well is [[Product Sense and Judgment]].

Lenny's metaphor lands here: PMs become **"editors of super-intelligent suggestions."** The model produces; you decide what's good, what ships, and what gets killed. Editing requires *more* taste than writing, not less — anyone who's edited knows this.

## The durable core

Strip away everything AI touches and what's left? Lenny calls it the **"people and glue"** work — influence, taste, vision, the judgment to frame the right problem. Marty Cagan's point reinforces it: the [[The Four Big Product Risks|four big product risks]] (value, usability, feasibility, viability) predate AI and outlast it. AI helps you *test* risk faster; it doesn't decide which risk matters.

> [!warning] Don't over-read the "elevated" column
> "Judgment is elevated" does not mean "ignore the tools." The PM who refuses to touch AI because their value is "strategy" is the one who gets out-executed by a PM with equal judgment and ten times the throughput. You climb to higher ground *and* you use the tools. Both.
