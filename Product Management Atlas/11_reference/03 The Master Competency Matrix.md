# The Master Competency Matrix

This is the keystone of the Atlas: every one of the 4 areas and 12 competencies from [[The PM Competency Model]], with a row per competency and a column per level, summarizing what *good* looks like at each rung. It's the single-page version of the model — the grid I actually use to level and coach.

> [!tip] How to read it
> Levels run **[[Associate Product Manager|APM]] → [[Product Manager|PM]] → [[Senior Product Manager|Senior]] → [[Staff and Principal Product Manager|Staff/Principal]] → [[Director and VP of Product|VP]]**. The skill never changes; the **altitude** does — scope, autonomy, ambiguity, influence (see [[What Changes at Each Level]]). Read *across* a row to see a single competency escalate; read *down* a column to see what a level demands across the board. Nobody is uniformly high across all twelve — you're [[The T-Shaped PM and Knowing Your Shape|spiky]], and that's the design.

The escalation follows one pattern across all twelve: **self/feature scope → multi-feature and trade-off mastery → team ownership → cross-team and company influence → org culture-building.** Roughly, an APM does it for a feature with manager support; a PM does it independently for complex work and influences the team's roadmap; a Senior owns team outcomes and coaches; a Staff/Principal drives strategy and reallocates investment; a VP builds the culture and the company-wide process. The scoring rubric underneath each cell is simple: **2+ Outperform · 1–1.5 On Track · 0–0.5 Needs Focus** (see [[The Proficiency Scale]]).

## Area 1 — [[Product Execution]]
*Define, build, and launch exceptional products.*

| Competency | APM | PM | Senior | Staff/Principal | VP |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Feature Specification]]** | Writes a clear spec for a *minor* feature; contributes to major ones; defines an achievable goal. | Specs *major, complex* functionality independently; finds the right level of detail; uses comps and prototypes. | Sequences *multiple interrelated* specs; considers all devices and points of sale; estimates effort into requirements. | Reviews the team's specs and coaches to clarity; improves the *spec process itself*. | Owns continuous improvement of how the whole org writes and reviews specs. |
| **[[Product Delivery]]** | Coordinates with the immediate team on a feature; communicates clearly; delivers small scope to plan. | Drives delivery of complex work independently; involves design/eng/business early; minimal surprises. | Coordinates *across* interdependent teams; explains trade-offs; protects the schedule under pressure. | Improves delivery practices for the team; unblocks systemic delivery issues; models the bar. | Builds the company's delivery operating rhythm and cadence. |
| **[[Product Quality]]** | Identifies and reports quality issues; resolves simple ones; learns the standard. | Owns quality for a complex feature across devices/use cases; classifies and prioritizes issues. | Sets the quality bar for the team's area; balances [[Sound Product Decision-Making\|decisions]] on impact vs effort. | Coaches quality judgment; raises the *definition* of good across teams. | Owns the org's quality philosophy and the systems that enforce it. |

## Area 2 — [[Customer Insight]]
*Understand and fulfill customer needs.*

| Competency | APM | PM | Senior | Staff/Principal | VP |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Fluency with Data]]** | Pulls and reads basic metrics; runs simple analyses with help. See [[Product Analytics and Metrics]]. | Designs and reads [[Experimentation and A-B Testing\|A/B tests]] with valid sample sizes; turns data into actions. | Connects metrics to business outcomes across an area; spots causation vs correlation. | Coaches analytical rigor; builds the team's measurement discipline. | Sets how the org instruments, measures, and learns. |
| **[[Voice of the Customer]]** | Sits in on research; captures feedback; learns the personas. See [[User Research and Discovery]]. | Runs [[Continuous Discovery\|discovery]] for a complex area; translates research into improvements. | Shapes strategy from synthesized insight across the area; advocates user needs in prioritization. | Builds the team's discovery habit and research standard. | Embeds customer-centricity as org culture. |
| **[[User Experience Design]]** | Gives basic design feedback; learns the product's UX patterns. See [[Design Sense and Critique]]. | Partners with design on complex flows; balances usability, feasibility, impact. | Drives UX coherence across an area; champions simplicity and consistency. | Elevates the team's [[Product Sense and Judgment\|design judgment]]; sets UX standards. | Owns the org's design bar and craft culture. |

## Area 3 — [[Product Strategy]]
*Deliver business impact via product innovation.*

| Competency | APM | PM | Senior | Staff/Principal | VP |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Business Outcome Ownership]]** | Connects a feature to a goal; tracks whether it moved. | Owns outcomes for a complex initiative; root-causes shortfalls and adjusts. | Owns the *team's* outcomes; reallocates effort toward leverage. | Owns outcomes across multiple teams; drives the [[Outcomes Over Outputs\|outcome culture]]. | Owns a P&L-scale set of outcomes for the org. |
| **[[Strategic Impact]]** | Understands the team's strategy; executes against it. | Contributes to the area's strategy; frames work in business terms. See [[The DHM Model]]. | Defines strategy for the area; synthesizes market/user/competitive inputs. | Sets *cross-team* strategy; influences company direction. | Sets and owns company product strategy. |
| **[[Product Vision and Roadmapping]]** | Maintains a feature-level roadmap; prioritizes with help. See [[RICE and Scoring Models]]. | Builds and manages an outcome-focused [[Building and Managing a Roadmap\|roadmap]] for a complex area. | Defines a [[Crafting a Product Vision\|vision]] for the area and a prioritized multi-quarter roadmap. | Drives portfolio-level vision; reallocates investment across teams. | Owns and evangelizes the company-wide product vision. |

## Area 4 — [[Influencing People]]
*Rally people around the team's work.*

| Competency | APM | PM | Senior | Staff/Principal | VP |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Stakeholder Management]]** | Identifies stakeholders for a feature; keeps them informed. | Manages stakeholders for a complex area; resolves conflict with empathy. See [[Internal and External Stakeholders]]. | Aligns senior stakeholders across an area; manages external partners credibly. | Aligns cross-team and exec stakeholders; sets the comms standard. | Manages company-level and external/market stakeholders. |
| **[[Team Leadership]]** | *Not applicable* — no reports; leads through example. | Begins informal leadership; mentors juniors; influences the team's roadmap. | Coaches peers and reports; owns team accountability for [[Outcomes Over Outputs\|outcomes]]. See [[Coaching and Feedback]]. | Drives product-wide influence; improves processes, hiring, and standards. See [[Hiring and Building a Product Team]]. | Builds the product culture; develops leaders; owns org design. |
| **[[Managing Up]]** | Keeps their manager informed; asks for help well. | Uses senior managers to unblock; frames asks in business terms. | Influences exec direction for the area; navigates competing interests. See [[Executive Communication]]. | Shapes company strategy through exec relationships; builds exec trust. | Operates *as* the executive; sets the context others manage up to. |

> [!note] Team Leadership starts late on purpose
> In the source model, [[Team Leadership]] is **"Not applicable" at the APM level** — you can't manage and mentor reports you don't have. It begins *informally* at PM (mentoring, roadmap influence) and only becomes a load-bearing competency from Senior up. This is one reason a "title is not a level" — see [[Myths and Misconceptions]].

## Using the matrix to assess and grow

This grid is a map, not a verdict. To turn it into action:

1. **Score yourself** down the rows with [[The Competency Self-Assessment]] and the [[The Proficiency Scale]].
2. **Find your shape** — the three or four spikes and the gaps ([[Identifying Growth Areas]]).
3. **Build a plan** against the lowest few, not the highest ([[Building a Growth Plan]]).
4. **Calibrate honestly** — leveling is evidence-based, not vibes ([[Score-Gated Leveling]], [[Calibration and Promotion]]).
