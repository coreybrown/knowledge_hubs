# 📚 Reference

This is the quick-lookup layer of the Atlas — the shelf you reach for when you don't want to read an essay, you want an answer. The other domains argue a point of view; these notes mostly don't. They flatten the model into tables you can scan, define the words I use elsewhere, hand you copy-paste skeletons, and answer the questions I get asked most. The opinions are still mine where it matters — the canon and the myths are deliberately argued — but the matrix and the glossary are built to be neutral and correct, not persuasive.

> [!tip] 🔎 Looking for one thing fast?
> The single most useful page here is [[The Master Competency Matrix]] — the entire [[The PM Competency Model|competency model]] in one grid. If you only bookmark one note in the Atlas, bookmark that.

## How this connects to the rest of the Atlas

Everything here is a compression of work argued elsewhere.

- The matrix and quick reference are the one-page versions of [[The PM Competency Model]] and [[The Product Career Ladder]].
- The glossary is the index into the [[The Frameworks Toolkit|frameworks]], the [[Customer Insight|discovery]] notes, and the [[Product Strategy|strategy]] notes.
- The canon, myths, FAQ, and tool landscape lean on [[Character, Competency, and Craft]] — my claim that tools and techniques are the *easy-to-teach* layer, and character is the hard one.
- The AI threads run through all of it; the deeper treatment is [[AI and the Modern PM]].

> [!note] A reference, not a substitute
> A grid can tell you *what* good looks like at a level. It can't tell you *how* to get there — that's what the competency and coaching notes are for. Use these pages to orient, then follow the links into the real material.
