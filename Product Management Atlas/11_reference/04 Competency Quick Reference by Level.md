# Competency Quick Reference by Level

The one-page cheat sheet for the ladder. For each rung from [[Associate Product Manager|APM]] to [[The Chief Product Officer|CPO]], here's the headline expectation across the four dials that actually differentiate levels: **Scope, Autonomy, Ambiguity, and Influence** (see [[What Changes at Each Level]]). The competencies don't change. The altitude does.

> [!tip] The four dials
> **Scope** = how much surface you're responsible for (feature → area → portfolio → company). **Autonomy** = how much direction you need (execute → set your plan → set strategy). **Ambiguity** = how defined the problem is when it reaches you (given → ill-defined → you define it). **Influence** = how far your decisions reach (team → org → market). The IC track scales ambiguity and influence *without* headcount; the manager track scales *through* people.

## The IC track

| Level | Scope | Autonomy | Ambiguity | Influence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Associate Product Manager\|APM]]** | A feature or small slice. | General supervision; routine, well-defined work. | Low: problems arrive scoped. | The immediate team. |
| **[[Product Manager\|PM]]** | A product area; complex, end-to-end features. | Limited supervision; sets own priorities within direction. | Moderate: owns ambiguous features, not the area's direction. | The immediate team and close partners. |
| **[[Senior Product Manager\|Senior PM]]** | A significant area with interdependencies. | Functions independently; a role model on the team. | High: defines the *how* and often the *what* for the area. | Across teams; influences exec decisions for the area. |
| **[[Staff and Principal Product Manager\|Staff/Principal]]** | Cross-team or portfolio-level problems. | Strong autonomy; defines context and leads through influence, not authority. | Very high: frames problems others don't yet see. | Company-wide; a recognized internal (then external) authority. |

The fork opens after Senior PM. The senior IC track (Staff → Principal → Distinguished) scales *influence and ambiguity* with no reports. See [[The IC and Manager Tracks]] and [[The IC vs Manager Decision]].

## The manager track

| Level | Scope | Autonomy | Ambiguity | Influence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Group Product Manager\|GPM]]** | One function/squad of PMs; translates objectives into operating plans. | General direction; manages the day-to-day for a team. | Moderate-high: turns strategy into executable plans. | A team of PMs and their partners. |
| **[[Director and VP of Product\|Director]]** | A department; multiple teams and multilayered managers. | Consultative direction; implements strategy across teams. | High: leads complex, cross-team problems to focused solutions. | The department and cross-functional peers. |
| **[[Director and VP of Product\|VP of Product]]** | A whole product org or major business line. | Full autonomy; leads through a management team. | Very high: contributes to *business* strategy, not just product. | The org and executive leadership. |
| **[[The Chief Product Officer\|CPO]]** | The company's entire product function. | Sets the strategic direction others execute against. | Highest: defines the problems the company chooses to solve. | The company and the market. |

## Two things this table won't tell you

> [!warning] A title is not a level
> These rows describe the *altitude* a level operates at. Plenty of people carry a title above (or below) the altitude they actually work at. Leveling has to be evidence-based, measured against [[The Master Competency Matrix]] rather than inferred from the business card. See [[Calibration and Promotion]] and the [[Myths and Misconceptions|myths]].

> [!note] You climb by *changing* the work, not adding to it
> The most common reason people stall is doing the previous level's job very well: a Senior still spec'ing single features instead of sequencing a roadmap, a new GPM still doing the work instead of multiplying it through people. Promotion means *operating differently*, not *more*. This is the whole point of [[The Six Stages of Mastery]].
