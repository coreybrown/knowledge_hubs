# How to Use This Knowledge Base

The Atlas is a connected web, not a linear book — but it rewards a starting point. Pick the path that matches where you are.

## Read it by where you are

> [!example] Pick your path
> - **"Is PM even for me?"** → [[What Is Product Management]] → [[Types of Product Managers]] → [[Foundations of the Craft]].
> - **"I'm an APM/PM leveling up."** → [[The PM Competency Model]] → run [[The Competency Self-Assessment]] → go deep on your two weakest competencies → [[Building a Growth Plan]].
> - **"I manage PMs."** → [[Assessment and Coaching]] → [[Score-Gated Leveling]] → [[The Manager as Coach]] → [[Calibration and Promotion]].
> - **"What does AI do to this job?"** → [[How AI Is Reshaping Product Management]] → [[Competencies AI Commoditizes vs Elevates]] → [[The AI Product Manager]].
> - **"I need a specific framework, now."** → [[The Frameworks Toolkit]] or [[Prioritization Frameworks]].

## How the notes are built

Every note is 350–700 words, cross-linked, and ends with a **Continue Reading** list so you can follow a thread. The ten domains after this one each open with a **hub note** (like [[Product Execution]] or [[The Product Career Ladder]]) that maps everything inside it. If you'd rather browse the whole structure at once, the [[Home]] page and [[The Master Competency Matrix]] are your overviews.

The four pillar domains — [[Product Execution]], [[Customer Insight]], [[Product Strategy]], [[Influencing People]] — are the heart. Each of their twelve competency notes follows the same shape:

1. **What it is** — my definition of the competency.
2. **The behaviors** — what it actually looks like in practice.
3. **How it shows up by level** — from [[Associate Product Manager|APM]] to VP.
4. **How to grow it** — concrete, opinionated coaching.
5. **How AI is changing it** — because in 2026, that section isn't optional.

## A note on the voice

This guide is **opinionated on purpose**. I state what I believe and why, because a reference that refuses to take a position is useless when you actually have to decide something. Where I'm giving you my take rather than settled fact, you'll know. Where popular belief is a [[Myths and Misconceptions|myth]], I say so. Disagree freely — knowing *why* you disagree is itself a sign your [[Product Sense and Judgment|product judgment]] is developing.

## The honest caveats

This is a map, not the territory. No framework survives contact with a real org chart, a real deadline, and a real executive who's already decided. Use the competencies as a shared language for growth, not a rubric to hide behind — and treat [[The Frameworks Toolkit]] as tools to reach for, never boxes to fill in. The "Ask the Guide" question box (press `/`) will answer in plain language from these notes if you'd rather just ask.
