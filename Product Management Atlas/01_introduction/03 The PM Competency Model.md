# The PM Competency Model

This is the spine of the whole Atlas. Over years of leveling and coaching PMs, I converged on a model with four competency **areas**, each holding three **competencies** — twelve in total. It's adapted from the Reforge / Ravi Mehta "Peak Product Manager" model, which is the closest thing our field has to a complete definition of the job. What I've added is the part that makes it usable: a verbatim set of **behaviors** under each competency, a **career ladder** mapped to it, and a **leveling system** that actually gates promotions ([[Score-Gated Leveling]]).

## The four areas and twelve competencies

| Area | What it means | The three competencies |
|---|---|---|
| **[[Product Execution]]** | Define, build, and launch exceptional products | [[Feature Specification]] · [[Product Delivery]] · [[Product Quality]] |
| **[[Customer Insight]]** | Understand and fulfill customer needs | [[Fluency with Data]] · [[Voice of the Customer]] · [[User Experience Design]] |
| **[[Product Strategy]]** | Deliver business impact via product innovation | [[Business Outcome Ownership]] · [[Strategic Impact]] · [[Product Vision and Roadmapping]] |
| **[[Influencing People]]** | Rally people around the team's work | [[Stakeholder Management]] · [[Team Leadership]] · [[Managing Up]] |

There's a rough arc here: execution is where most careers start and strategy/influence is where they mature — but that's a center of gravity, not a rule. Which brings me to the most important idea in the model.

## You are spiky, not well-rounded

> [!tip] Know your shape
> Almost nobody is strong across all twelve. The best PMs I've worked with are **spiky** — exceptional at three or four competencies, competent at the rest — and they win by knowing their shape and building teams that fill the gaps. Chasing a flat, uniformly "high" profile is a way to be mediocre at everything. More in [[The T-Shaped PM and Knowing Your Shape]].

## Levels: the same competencies, a higher bar

The twelve competencies don't change as you climb; the **altitude** does. I map progress on a six-stage mastery spine — from *Gaining Experience* to *Recognized Thought Leader* — detailed in [[The Six Stages of Mastery]]. The same skill (say, [[Feature Specification]]) means "writes a clear spec for one feature with help" at the [[Associate Product Manager|APM]] level and "improves the spec process for every team" at the VP level. The full progression lives in [[The Product Career Ladder]] and the at-a-glance grid in [[The Master Competency Matrix]].

## Where this model is thin (and how I patch it)

I'd rather be honest than dogmatic. A clean 4×12 grid under-weights a few things, so I treat these as first-class even though they don't sit neatly in one box: **discovery** as its own discipline ([[Continuous Discovery]], [[The Four Big Product Risks]]), **product sense** ([[Product Sense and Judgment]]), technical and **GTM** fluency, and—loudest of all right now—the way **AI is rewriting which competencies even matter** ([[Competencies AI Commoditizes vs Elevates]]).
