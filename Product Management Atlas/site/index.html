In one paragraph

So — what is product management?

Product management is the discipline of being accountable for whether a product succeeds — not for shipping features, but for the outcomes they produce. The job lives where user value, business viability, usability, and feasibility meet, and the real work is judgment under uncertainty: deciding which problems are worth solving and rallying a team to solve them. I organize that ability into four areas and twelve competencies — Product Execution, Customer Insight, Product Strategy, and Influencing People — that escalate from APM to CPO. This Atlas maps all of it, including how AI is rewriting the craft.
