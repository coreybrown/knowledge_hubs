Product Execution

Specs, delivery, quality, and my 10-50-90 framework — the work of shipping.

3 min read·721 words
●The definition I use

Product Execution — Define, build, and launch exceptional products.

This is the first of the four competency areas in The PM Competency Model, and I put it first on purpose. Execution is the foundation the entire PM career is built on. Strategy, vision, influence — all of it is theory until something ships and changes a number. I've watched brilliant strategists wash out because they couldn't get anything across the line, and I've watched relentless executors get promoted past them. The order is not an accident.

#My point of view: execution is the one thing a PM can never be bad at

You can be spiky everywhere else. The competency model is explicit that you should be — nobody is great at all twelve, and chasing a flat profile is a recipe for being mediocre at everything (The T-Shaped PM and Knowing Your Shape). But execution is the exception I'll fight for. A PM who can't define a clear spec, ship to plan, and hold a quality bar isn't a junior PM — they're not yet doing the job. Everything else is a multiplier on a base of can this person reliably get good product out the door. If the base is zero, the multiplier doesn't matter.

This is also why execution dominates the early ladder. At APM and PM levels, you are largely judged on execution — can you spec a feature, work with engineering, and ship it without it falling over. Strategy and influence are where careers mature (The Product Career Ladder), but they're built on top of an execution reputation you earn in years one through four.

#The three competencies

This area breaks into three competencies, each with its own note and named behaviors drawn from my model.

CompetencyWhat it coversThe behaviors
Feature SpecificationTurning a problem into shared, buildable understandingOutcome Oriented · Comprehensive Documentation · Clarity
Product DeliveryWorking with the team to actually shipTeam Coordination & Alignment · Communication · Delivery to Plan
Product QualityHolding the bar on decisions and riskSound Product Decision Making · Risk Identification & Mitigation

#Where execution connects

Execution doesn't live in a vacuum. Good specs are downstream of good discovery — you can't define what to build until you understand the problem and the four big risks. What you choose to execute on is a prioritization decision. And the whole point of shipping is to move a business outcome, which is why Outcomes Over Outputs sits at the end of this domain rather than the start of the strategy one.

▲The trap at the top

Senior PMs often try to "graduate" from execution into pure strategy. The best leaders I've worked with never do. They stop doing the spec themselves and start raising the team's execution bar — better processes, better reviews, fewer surprises. The work changes altitude; it never stops being your responsibility.
