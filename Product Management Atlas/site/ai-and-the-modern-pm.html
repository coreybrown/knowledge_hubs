AI and the Modern PM

Evals, vibe-coding, AI product sense, and the competencies AI is rewriting.

3 min read·555 words

This is the domain I care about most, because it's the one that's actively rewriting the job description while you read this. Everything else in the Atlas — the twelve competencies, the ladder, the frameworks — still holds. AI doesn't replace that spine. It changes which parts of it are scarce. This is my read, as someone currently building products with AI, on where the job is going and what to do about it.

#The thesis

✦My position

The PMs who win the next five years are the ones who become builders again. The mechanical core of the job — drafting specs, summarizing research, writing status updates, basic analysis — is commoditizing fast. The premium is moving to judgment, taste, problem framing, and the new technical literacy of shipping probabilistic systems. AI doesn't reduce the work; it raises the floor and the ceiling at the same time.

Lenny Rachitsky argues PMs are best positioned to thrive in the AI era, and I think he's right — conditionally. The condition is adaptation. The PM whose value was "I write the doc and run the meeting" is in trouble. The PM whose value was "I decide what's worth building and why" just got a force multiplier. Same title, opposite trajectories.

I'll be blunt about the hype: "PM is dead" is clickbait (more in Myths and Misconceptions). The honest version is that the commodity PM is dying and the judgment PM is compounding.

#What's actually new

A few skills crossed from nice-to-have to table stakes. The biggest one: evals. OpenAI's Kevin Weil calls writing evals a core PM skill, and I agree — it's the new PRD. If you take one thing from this domain, make it Writing Evals for AI Products.

#A note on the numbers

Throughout this domain I cite salary and adoption figures (AI PM comp, "20% of PM jobs," and so on). Treat all of it as directional. The market is moving monthly, sources disagree, and Levels.fyi is a snapshot, not gospel. I use the numbers to show direction and magnitude, not to promise a specific paycheck.
