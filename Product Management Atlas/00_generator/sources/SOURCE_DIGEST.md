## 1. Product Level Competency Matrix — DRAFT v1 (PRIMARY MODEL — the spine)

Corey's own model (built at PENN; explicitly derived from Reforge / Ravi Mehta). **4 Competency Areas → 12 Competencies → behaviors**, mapped across two ladders (IC + Manager) via a shared 6-stage mastery progression.

**Scoring rubric (per behavior/competency):** `2+: Outperform | 1–1.5: On Track | 0–0.5: Needs Focus`

### The 4 Areas (verbatim)
1. **Product Execution** — *Define, build, and launch exceptional products*
2. **Customer Insight** — *Understand and fulfill customer needs*
3. **Product Strategy** — *Deliver business impact via product innovation*
4. **Influencing People** — *Rally people around the team's work*

### The 12 Competencies (verbatim definitions)

**Product Execution**
1. **Feature Specification** — The ability for a PM to gather requirements, define functionality, and set goals in a clear, actionable format that can be used to communicate with the team and drive product delivery.
2. **Product Delivery** — The ability to work closely with one's immediate team (engineering, design, etc.) to iteratively and quickly deliver product functionality that accomplishes pre-defined goals.
3. **Product Quality** — The ability to identify, prioritize, and resolve technical, functional, and business quality issues across all devices, points of sale, and use cases that are applicable to the product.

**Customer Insight**
4. **Fluency with Data** — The ability to use data to generate actionable insights, to leverage those insights to achieve goals set for the product, and to connect those quantified goals to meaningful outcomes for the business.
5. **Voice of the Customer** — The ability to leverage user feedback (usability tests, focus groups, surveys, other research) to understand how users engage with the product, make better decisions, and drive meaningful outcomes for the business.
6. **User Experience Design** — The ability, individually and with the design team, to define requirements and deliver UX designs that are easy to use, leverage UX best practices, and dovetail with the product's predominant UX patterns.

**Product Strategy**
7. **Business Outcome Ownership** — The ability to drive meaningful outcomes for the business by connecting product functionality and goals to the strategic objectives of the team and company.
8. **Strategic Impact** — The ability to understand and contribute to the business strategy for the team and company, and to bring strategy to fruition through consistent delivery of business outcomes.
9. **Product Vision and Roadmapping** — The ability to define a vision for the PM's product area that connects to team/company strategy, and to define a clear roadmap of highly prioritized features/initiatives that deliver against that vision.

**Influencing People**
10. **Stakeholder Management** — The ability to proactively identify stakeholders impacted by the PM's area and to work with them to factor their requirements into product decisions.
11. **Team Leadership** — The ability to manage and mentor direct reports so they deliver on their product areas, improve against these competencies, deliver outcomes, and achieve career objectives.
12. **Managing Up** — The ability to leverage senior managers/executives to help achieve goals, deliver outcomes, and positively influence the strategic direction of the team and company.

### Behaviors under each competency (Corey's words — verbatim)

- **Feature Specification:** *Outcome Oriented* (value/impact over implementation; the 'why' applied throughout) · *Comprehensive Documentation* (problem, objective, scope=user stories+acceptance criteria, UX/tech considerations, success metrics; edge cases, data, risks, GTM) · *Clarity* (concise, easy to align across teams; organized decisions; clear impacts/actions/next steps).
- **Product Delivery:** *Team Coordination & Alignment* (efforts tied to objectives; involve design/eng/business early; effective meetings; explain the 'why' behind trade-offs) · *Communication* (clear/concise/empathetic across formats; right method; tailored to audience; active listening) · *Delivery to Plan* (delivered to requirements within eng's estimate; clear schedule/rollout/scope; minimal surprises).
- **Product Quality:** *Sound Product Decision Making* (qual+quant data; real user needs; balance impact/effort/alignment; collaborate for input; accountable, timely decisions) · *Risk Identification & Mitigation* (early detection; classify by impact×likelihood; contingency plans; transparent comms; ongoing monitoring).
- **Fluency with Data:** *Feature Evaluation & A/B Testing* (clear hypotheses; valid sample sizes/minimal bias; interpret conversion/retention w/ stat-sig; roll out/iterate/sunset) · *Actionable Insight Generation* (ask the right questions; interpret in context; trends/anomalies/causation-not-correlation; translate to opportunities; present to drive action).
- **Voice of the Customer:** *User Persona & Journeys Comprehension* (goals/contexts/pain via UXR/surveys; collaborate with UX/research; identify unmet needs) · *User Insights Application* (translate research to improvements; advocate user needs in prioritization/design; shape strategy).
- **User Experience Design:** *Design Sense & Feedback* (advocate intuitive user-centered design; clear context/feedback/problem framing; balance usability/feasibility/impact; champion consistency & simplicity; iterate from feedback) · *Competitive Landscape Expertise* (monitor competitors/trends; analyze strengths/weaknesses/positioning; spot differentiation; translate to priorities/messaging; communicate market context).
- **Business Outcome Ownership:** *Product Delivery Impact* (initiatives that meaningfully move key business goals; reduce risk & unblock strategic work) · *Outcome Analysis & Actions* (evaluate outcomes vs goals; root-cause successes/shortfalls w/ data+qual; extract actionable takeaways; adjust strategy/execution).
- **Strategic Impact:** *Business Understanding & Clarity* (grasp core business model & bottom-line drivers; balance user value w/ viability & growth; communicate impact in business terms; anticipate industry risks & leverage points) · *Product Strategy Definition* (articulate strategy in user+business value; prioritized outcome-driven objectives; communicate to inspire/align; adapt while keeping long-term focus) · *Product Strategy Identification* (connect vision to high-impact areas via needs/value/gaps; synthesize market/user/competitive/internal inputs; prioritize strategies e.g. ROI; validate w/ stakeholders).
- **Product Vision and Roadmapping:** *Product Prioritization* (strategic lens: user value/business impact/feasibility; structured frameworks RICE/impact-effort/cost-benefit; balance short vs long term; transparent rationale; say 'no' to focus on highest leverage) · *Roadmap Creation & Management* (translate strategy → outcome-focused roadmap; align cross-functional early; tailor comms to audience; continuously review/adjust while preserving intent) · *Long-Term Vision Definition & Evangelism* (compelling long-term vision that inspires/aligns; anchored in customer/market/strategy; balance aspiration w/ feasibility; reinforce & evolve).
- **Stakeholder Management:** *Internal Stakeholder Influence & Alignment* (relationships via regular touchpoints; align goals/priorities/metrics; persuasive, audience-adapted comms; resolve conflict w/ empathy) · *External Stakeholder Management* (trusted relationships w/ customers/partners/vendors; translate external feedback to direction; represent company credibly; manage expectations transparently; create mutual value) · *Org-Based Communications* (articulate vision/strategy/priorities at all levels; adapt messaging; align via transparent updates; storytelling + data).
- **Team Leadership:** *Coaching & Performance* (actionable feedback via regular touchpoints; tailor to strengths/goals; model strong practices; safe space for learning) · *Team Impact* (align team goals to business; accountability for outcomes not activity; review progress; remove blockers/empower) · *Product-Wide Influence & Leadership* (company-wide advocate for PM; improve processes/tools/standards; invest in development/hiring; foster product culture; spread learning) · *Resource Management & Hiring* (define role expectations/competencies; attract diverse high-potential candidates; holistic evaluation; inclusive evidence-based decisions; align org structure to needs).
- **Managing Up:** *Strategic Influence & Alignment* (connect product vision to company strategy; rationale/storytelling/data to influence; navigate ambiguity & competing interests; champion shared outcomes; trust w/ exec functions) · *Executive Communications* (concise, strategic-impact-focused; frame in business terms tied to KPIs/ROI; data-backed; anticipate questions/risks; calm & solution-oriented).

> The **Manager Competency Matrix** uses the identical 4 areas / 12 competencies / behaviors; only the level columns change. Leveling is expressed via the ladder + mastery-spine mapping below.

### The two ladders
**IC ladder (8):** Product Manager I → PM II → PM III → Sr PM → Staff PM → Sr Staff PM → Principal PM (source spells "Principle") → Distinguished PM.
**Manager ladder (6–7):** (Supervisor) → Group PM (GPM) → Sr Manager → Director → Sr Director → VP → SVP. *(Baseline sheet also inserts a VP II between VP and SVP.)*

### The 6-stage mastery spine (shared, verbatim)
Gaining Experience → Applies Practical Knowledge → Applies Advanced Knowledge → Subject Matter Expert → Master of Discipline → Recognized Thought Leader (Inside/Outside the Org).

### Job-Architecture mapping (IC) — title · grade · years · COMPLEXITY / AUTONOMY
- **PM I** (Prof 1, 0+ yrs) — *Complexity:* assist in market research, support dev, help create product docs; collaborate to gather requirements. *Autonomy:* general supervision, dependent on others, routine work, limited scope.
- **PM II** (Prof 2, 2+) — market analysis, define requirements, manage lifecycle; work w/ eng/marketing/sales for launches. *Autonomy:* limited supervision, begins to impact projects.
- **PM III** (Prof 3, 4+) — lead dev projects, prioritize from feedback, analyze performance; mentor juniors, contribute to planning. *Autonomy:* general direction but sets own priorities; impacts multiple projects.
- **Sr PM** (Prof 4, 6+) — develop & execute strategies, drive innovation, technical leadership; align w/ execs. *Autonomy:* functions independently, team role model.
- **Staff PM** (Prof 5, 8+) — manage timelines/deliverables, ensure quality; key-stakeholder relationships. *Autonomy:* strong autonomy, mentors, defines context, leads projects forward.
- **Sr Staff PM** (Prof 6, 10+) — set vision for PM initiatives, drive methodology innovation, represent PM at exec level. *Autonomy:* independent leadership through teams, mentors others.
- **Principal PM** (Prof 7, 12+) — define overall product strategy, oversee all product activities, industry thought leader. *Autonomy:* fully autonomous, recognized thought leader & strategic advisor; leads high-value initiatives.

### Job-Architecture mapping (Manager) — COMPLEXITY / AUTONOMY
- **GPM/Supervisor** (Mgmt 1–2, 6+) — set priorities; translate objectives → operational plans; build cohesive operating rhythms. *Autonomy:* general direction, manages day-to-day for one function.
- **Sr Manager** (Mgmt 3, 6+) — clarify direction, keep work aligned; adaptive development, sustainable culture. *Autonomy:* leads projects + day-to-day for managers/employees in one function.
- **Director** (Mgmt 4, 8+) — implements strategy; leads complex issues → focused solutions; collaborative cross-functional process across dept. *Autonomy:* consultative direction across multiple teams; directs multilayered managers.
- **Sr Director** (Mgmt 5, 10+) — moves from implementing → setting functional strategy; impacts org objectives & long-term performance; inspires commitment. *Autonomy:* sets strategic direction across functions; begins to impact orgs outside own; manages multilayered directors.
- **VP** (Mgmt 6, 12+) — executes functional business plans, contributes to functional/business strategy; collaborates w/ exec leadership. *Autonomy:* full autonomy; leads through a management team.
- **SVP** (Mgmt 7, 15+) — leads/guides/evaluates other executives (VPs, Sr Directors). *Autonomy:* full autonomy; sets org-initiative strategic direction.

## 2. PM Evaluation Framework Sandbox (Corey's self-assessment tool)

Adapted from the Almundo / Mind the Product talent-growth model. **9 skills × 3 categories**, scored 1–5, with per-level targets + growth plan.

**Categories & skills:** *Holistic Comprehension* → Product Thinking, UX, Business/Product Strategy · *Processes* → Strategy Execution, Agile, Data-Informed · *Management* → Leadership, Communication, Proactivity.

**The 1–5 scale (verbatim):** 1 = Inadequate–Gap · 2 = Supervised–Knows · 3 = Unsupervised–Does · 4 = Generates–Masters · 5 = Leads–Teaches.

**Per-level target totals (max 45):** APM 14 · PM 22 · SPM 30 · LPM 38. (Communication & Proactivity carry the highest per-level bar.) Per-skill targets rise APM→LPM; e.g. Communication 2/3/4/5, Proactivity 3/3/4/5.

**Growth plan structure:** pick the 3 lowest skills → 3 "small tasks" each (Growth Area #1/#2/#3 × Small Task 1/2/3).

## 3. theScore Career Journey (Corey's score-gated leveling system)

**11 HARD skills:** Strategic Thinking & Product Planning · Delivering Against Product Goals/KPIs · Team Management & Accountability · Reporting & Stakeholder Management · User Science, Research & Empathy · Data Analysis & Experimentation · Process Implementation, Iteration & Improvement · Requirement & Documentation Building · Grasp of Product/Market/Competitor Ecosystem · Innovation & PM Community Building · Leadership Fundamentals. (max 55)

**7 SOFT skills:** Teamwork/Respect · Communication · Problem Solving · Efficiency & Accuracy · Attitude (Positive/Motivator/Advocate) · Initiative · Time Management. (max 35; grand total max 90)

**1–5 ratings:** 5 Master · 4 Advanced · 3 Good · 2 Minimum · 1 Need to improve.

**Score-gated levels (the gating logic — verbatim):**
| Role | Gate |
|---|---|
| APM | Score **43–54** |
| PM | **55–67** & no category below 2 & 1 yr as APM |
| SPM | **68–80** & no category below 3 & no required soft skill below 3.5 & 2 yrs as PM |
| LPM | **81+** & no required skill below 4 & 2 yrs as SPM |

Gating logic: score band (primary) + floor rules that tighten with level + additive tenure.

**Governance / appraisal (reusable, non-confidential):** Leads score their team; career-journey conversations begin as someone nears the top of a level; year-round transparency; level moves at yearly review if expectations met; increases approved up the chain. **Appraisal Do's:** focus on job-related feedback; give positive + constructive; first-hand evidence; constructive criticism. **Don'ts:** personal-personality comments; only-negative; rumors/allegations; sugarcoating. **Prep self-check:** considered strengths + weaknesses; can substantiate with specific examples; anticipated problems & reactions.

## 4. Hunt / Reforge canonical matrix (per-level prose, APM→VP)

The canonical "Peak Product Manager" matrix Corey's DRAFT v1 derives from. Levels: **APM → Early PM → Later PM → SPM → LPM → Sr. Director → VP**. (Renames "Product Quality"→"Product Quality Assurance"; splits PM into Early/Later.)

**General escalation pattern (all 12):** self/feature scope → multi-feature & trade-off mastery → team ownership → cross-team/company influence → org culture-building. Roughly: APM does it for a feature w/ manager support; Early/Later PM does it independently for complex work & influences the team roadmap; SPM owns team outcomes & coaches; LPM drives team strategy & reallocates investment; Sr Director influences company-level decisions & raises org skill; VP builds the culture & company-wide processes. (Notably: **Team Leadership is "Not applicable" at APM & Early PM** — it begins informally at Later PM.) ## 5. Supporting frameworks (PDFs/images — for the Frameworks Toolkit + foundations)

- **theScore Product Principles:** *Have a shooters mentality* (be bold, not afraid to fail) · *Balance game sense with data* (qual+quant+intuition; avoid analysis paralysis; inform the next iteration; don't beat a dead horse) · *We win with fans* (fan at the core; painkillers over vitamins; unify roadmaps; fans drive the bottom line) · *Talk it up* (ship-and-share; teach vs tell; breadcrumbs not loaves; first-15s rule). Anatomy: specific/actionable, short, spiky POV, motivational. NOT metrics/KPIs.
- **PM Wheel (Petra Wille):** 8 skills — Understand the problem · Find a solution · Do some planning · Get it done! · Listen & learn · Team · Grow! · Agile.
- **Initiative Pyramid (Curtis Stanier):** risk × number of initiatives; senior-leadership involvement consulted→informed (few high-risk = consulted; many low-risk = informed).
- **Roles by Title / Product×Design Venn:** PM = overlap of Product + Design + Engineering; "understanding trade-offs." Product∩UX overlap = service design, IA, research synthesis, stakeholder facilitation, concept modeling, user stories.
- **Skill Coaching × Effort (hard→easy to teach):** Character traits (humility, empathy, integrity, mindset, positivity, decisiveness, resilience, values) → Competencies (leadership, communication, collaboration, execution, technical, critical thinking, analysis, creativity, strategy) → Techniques (user stories, backlog, research, data viz, working w/ eng, stakeholders, status updates, SQL) → Processes (Scrum, Kanban, OKRs, JTBD, Kano, Product Kata, Design Sprint) → Tools (Jira, Trello, Asana, FullStory, Optimizely, Balsamiq, Amplitude, Intercom, Typeform).
- **Impact × Likelihood 2×2:** Strategic Initiatives (high impact / low likelihood) · Home Runs (high/high) · Housekeeping (low/high) · Stuff You Shouldn't Be Doing (low/low).
- **Eisenhower 2×2:** urgent×important → do now / decide-when / delegate / do-later.
- **Product Organization Matrix:** Outcome vs Feature teams × Owner vs Facilitator → PM as Owner (accountable) vs Facilitator (brings people together). Echoes Cagan's product-vs-feature-teams.
