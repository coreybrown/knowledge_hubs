# External PM Competency Frameworks — Research Synthesis (validation layer)

> Validates/challenges the KB's spine (Reforge / Ravi Mehta 4-pillar model). Used to identify gaps to layer in. Full citations preserved. Researched June 2026.

## 1. Ravi Mehta / Reforge — the model the KB adapts
- 4 pillars: **Product Execution, Customer Insight, Product Strategy, Influencing People**; 12 sub-competencies (3 each). Mehta calls it the closest thing to a MECE definition of PM.
- 12: Feature Specification, Product Delivery, Product Quality / Fluency with Data, Voice of the Customer, User Experience Design / Business Outcome Ownership, Product Vision & Roadmapping, Strategic Impact / Stakeholder Management, Team Leadership, Managing Up.
- Assessment scale = **Needs Focus → On Track → Outperform** (3-tier). Ships an APM→VP per-level matrix. (CONFIRMS Corey's DRAFT v1 headers.)
- "What's Your Shape?": most PMs are **spiky**, not uniformly strong; great products = spiky people on well-rounded teams. Product Builders vs Architects; PMs vs Product Leaders.
- Sources: reforge.com/blog/product-manager-skills · ravi-mehta.com/product-manager-roles · blog.ravi-mehta.com/p/product-competency-toolkit · reforge.com/guides/assess-your-team-with-the-pm-competency-model

## 2. Marty Cagan / SVPG — INSPIRED/EMPOWERED
- PM at intersection of customer needs, business objectives, tech possibilities; deep knowledge of user/data/business/market. "Competent people with **character**."
- **4 big product risks**: Value, Usability, Feasibility, Business Viability. Ownership: PM = value+viability, Designer = usability, Tech Lead = feasibility.
- **Product vs feature teams** (outcomes & empowered vs output & not); missionaries vs mercenaries. PM ≠ Product Owner (PO ≈ 10% of PM); never split discovery from delivery.
- **Product leader** = management (coaching + staffing) + leadership (strategic context/vision/strategy). Coaching = #1 manager responsibility (coaching+staffing ~80% of a first-level manager's week).
- svpg.com/four-big-risks · /product-vs-feature-teams · /product-manager-vs-product-owner-revisited

## 3. Petra Wille — Strong Product People / PMwheel
- 8 dimensions: Understand the problem, Find a solution, Do some planning, Get it done, Listen & learn, Team, Grow!, Agile. 0–7 self-assessment radar.
- A **coaching/career-conversation** tool; PLwheel extends to product leadership. "Great product leaders make strong product people."
- petra-wille.com/blog/how-to-use-the-pmwheel-as-a-self-assessment-tool-for-product-managers · /plwheel

## 4. Lenny Rachitsky — skills, great PM, leveling
- Top 3 skills in order: **communication → execution → product sense**. Execution is the floor; "I got this" aura. 3 roles: build the right product, drive execution, represent/communicate.
- Compiled career ladders from 20+ companies; PM "inconsistently defined" → ladders matter.
- lennysnewsletter.com/p/product-management-career-ladders · /how-to-develop-product-sense · maven.com/articles/product-manager-habits

## 5. Other models
- **Brandon Chu** (Black Box of PM): "maximize impact to the mission"; PM as **"the API"** across disciplines. blackboxofpm.com
- **Shreyas Doshi**: 3 levels of product work — **Impact / Execution / Optics**; "most execution problems are strategy problems"; **LNO** effort framework (Leverage/Neutral/Overhead).
- **Gibson Biddle**: **DHM** — strategy should Delight, be Hard-to-copy, Margin-enhancing.
- **John Cutler**: **"feature factory"** anti-pattern; **North Star Framework** (one value metric + inputs).
- **Teresa Torres**: **Continuous Discovery** (weekly customer touchpoints by the team) + **Opportunity Solution Tree**; the **product trio** (PM+design+eng).
- **T-shaped** (Guest 1991, IDEO/Tim Brown): depth × breadth → parent of Mehta's "shape."
- **Google APM** (Mayer, 2002): template for APM programs.

## 6. Cross-model consensus (irreducible core)
1. Execution & delivery 2. Customer/market insight & discovery 3. Strategy & business outcomes 4. Influence & communication 5. (near-universal, often implicit) product sense/judgment + self-growth.
Shared tenets: outcomes over output; PM as "CEO of product" without authority → lateral influence; product trio; spiky/T-shaped on balanced teams.

## 7. Career ladders
- **IC track**: APM → PM → Senior PM → Staff PM → (Senior Staff) → Principal PM. (Atlassian worked example: 7 levels APM→Director.)
- **Manager track**: Group PM/GPM → Director → Senior Director → VP Product → CPO.
- **Fork** opens after Senior PM (IC→Principal vs mgr→GPM/Director).
- **4 escalation axes**: Scope (feature→portfolio→company), Autonomy (execute→set plan→set strategy), Ambiguity (defined→ill-defined→define-the-problem), Influence (team→org→market). IC ceiling scales influence/ambiguity WITHOUT headcount; manager track scales THROUGH people.

## 8. Gaps in the 4-pillar model → what to LAYER IN
The pillars are a sound, validated skeleton (clusters match field consensus; uniquely ships assessment+leveling). Blind spots are **practices & judgment**, not categories:
1. **Discovery** under-named (add Cagan 4 risks + Torres continuous discovery/OST).
2. **Technical fluency / feasibility** missing.
3. **Product sense / taste / judgment** implicit (Lenny: trainable, top-3).
4. **GTM / product marketing / commercialization** thin (Biddle margin/defensibility; Cagan viability).
5. **Communication** as its own named skill (Lenny #1; Chu "the API").
6. **Ethics / responsibility / trust & safety** absent.
7. **Self-growth / learning** absent (Wille "Grow!").
8. **Leadership craft for manager track** thins above Senior PM (Cagan EMPOWERED, Wille PLwheel).
9. **"Optics"** unrepresented (Shreyas).

**Net**: keep the 4×3 grid + shape/spiky framing; consciously layer in discovery, product sense, strategy depth (DHM), technical fluency, GTM, communication-as-skill, leadership craft, and ethics.
