# The PM Wheel

**Petra Wille's PM Wheel** (from *Strong Product People*) is a self-assessment radar that maps eight product-management skills around a circle, scored 0 to 7 each, so you can see the *shape* of your competence at a glance. I like it because it's deliberately a **coaching tool**, not a grading tool. Its whole reason for existing is to start a good career conversation, not to produce a number for a promotion packet.

## The eight skills

> [!note] The dimensions
> **Understand the problem**: discovery, user and market insight, framing what's actually worth solving.
> **Find a solution**: ideation, design judgment, separating good answers from clever ones.
> **Do some planning**: prioritization, roadmapping, sequencing.
> **Get it done!**: execution, delivery, shipping.
> **Listen & learn**: feedback loops, measurement, iterating on evidence.
> **Team**: collaboration, working through and with the people around you.
> **Grow!**: self-development, learning, deliberately getting better.
> **Agile**: fluency with iterative ways of working.

You rate each, plot the points, and connect them. The output is a shape, and the shape is the message.

## What the shape tells you

> [!tip] You're looking for spikes and gaps, not a big circle
> The instinct is to want a perfectly round, maxed-out wheel. That's the wrong goal. Great PMs are **spiky**, not uniformly excellent. They're exceptional at a few things and adequate at the rest, and great *products* come from spiky people on well-rounded *teams* (see [[The T-Shaped PM and Knowing Your Shape]]). The wheel's value is making your spikes and your genuine gaps visible so you can decide, deliberately, which to lean into and which to shore up or staff around.

A round wheel scored at 4 across the board isn't better than a spiky one. It often means either a generalist with no superpower or someone who hasn't self-assessed honestly. The diagnostic question isn't "is it round?" It's "is the shape *intentional*, and does it fit the role I'm in?"

## How it fits the rest of the Atlas

The PM Wheel is one of three lenses on the same underlying skill set. The Atlas's own [[The PM Competency Model|competency model]] is the rigorous, leveled version: four pillars, twelve competencies, behaviors mapped to a ladder. Petra Wille's wheel is the *lightweight, self-directed* counterpart: faster, friendlier, better for a quarterly self-check or a coaching chat. I use the formal model for [[Calibration and Promotion|calibration]] and the wheel for [[Identifying Growth Areas|spotting where to grow next]]. They don't compete; they operate at different ceremonies.

| Tool | Best for | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| PM Wheel (Wille) | Self-assessment, coaching chats | The PM |
| [[The PM Competency Model\|Competency Model]] | Leveling, calibration, reviews | Manager + PM |

> [!warning] Don't let a self-assessment masquerade as an evaluation
> The wheel is *self-scored*, which makes it honest fuel for reflection and a terrible instrument for promotion decisions. People rate themselves inconsistently: some sandbag, some inflate. The moment you try to compare two people's wheels or gate a level on a wheel score, you've broken the tool. Keep it as a mirror, not a measuring stick. For that, see [[The Competency Self-Assessment]] and [[The Proficiency Scale]].
